Navacaprant (NMRA-140) is Neumora Therapeutics' selective κ-opioid receptor antagonist for treatment-resistant depression. The firm reached a conditional advance verdict — compelling mechanism and strong capitalization, but the Phase 3 readout is the decisive gate before any position recommendation.

Verdict — Conditional Advance
Partners split 4–1 in favor of advancing to IND-enabling diligence, contingent on the Q3 2025 Phase 3 KOASTAL-3 readout. The dissent — filed by the Adversarial partner — flagged the Phase 2b MADRS miss as a mechanism signal, not sampling noise. Neumora's decision to proceed on the secondary anhedonia finding is a fundable thesis, not a demonstrated one.

The financial position is strong: Neumora closed a $399M Series C and received FDA Fast Track designation in December 2024. KOASTAL-1 missed its primary MADRS endpoint but showed a statistically significant anhedonia signal, which the Phase 3 program is powered to confirm as the primary outcome.
