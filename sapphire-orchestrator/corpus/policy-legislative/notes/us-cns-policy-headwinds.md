# US policy headwinds & signals for CNS drug development and pricing

The policy environment (dossier field **F3**) shapes what CNS programs cost, how fast they're reviewed,
and whether they're reimbursed. Four current US signals, all CNS-relevant.

## 1. Coverage gate on the anti-amyloid class (CMS CED, 2022→)
CMS finalized that "Medicare will cover monoclonal antibodies that target amyloid (or plaque) for the
treatment of Alzheimer's disease that receive traditional approval from the Food and Drug Administration
(FDA) under coverage with evidence development (CED)." [CMS, 7 Apr 2022 — cms.gov] **Impact:** Medicare
reimbursement for Leqembi/donanemab is conditioned on CMS-approved registry participation — an access/
pricing headwind unique to this CNS class.

## 2. Medicare price negotiation now reaches CNS drugs (IRA, 2nd cycle 2025)
The IRA's Drug Price Negotiation Program selected, for its second cycle (announced 17 Jan 2025; prices
effective 2027), two CNS medicines among 15 drugs: **Vraylar** (cariprazine; schizophrenia/bipolar) and
**Austedo** (deutetrabenazine; Huntington's chorea / tardive dyskinesia). [CMS — cms.gov] **Impact:**
marketed CNS franchises now face direct US price-setting once eligibility is met.

## 3. The "pill penalty" — a structural CNS small-molecule headwind (IRA)
Under the IRA, "high-spending drugs can be selected for negotiation if they are brand-name drugs or
biological products without generic or biosimilar equivalents, and at least 7 years (for small molecule
drugs) or 11 years (for biologics) past their FDA approval or licensure date." [KFF] **Impact:** the
4-year-shorter pre-negotiation window for small molecules is cited as reducing early-stage small-molecule
investment — disproportionately CNS, which is overwhelmingly small-molecule. The **EPIC Act (H.R. 1492)**
is the proposed fix (equalize at ~13 years). *(Statute is congress.gov — Cloudflare-blocked to automated
fetch this pass; cited via KFF, see manifest.)*

## 4. Accelerated-approval reform after Aduhelm (FDORA, 2022)
FDA's accelerated-approval program — reformed by FDORA (Section 3210 set up an Accelerated Approval
Coordinating Council) — still requires confirmatory trials to verify benefit; FDA's 2025 draft guidance
defines when a confirmatory trial must be "underway." [FDA — fda.gov] **Impact:** a higher bar for
surrogate-based CNS accelerated approvals (e.g. amyloid), and faster withdrawal if benefit isn't confirmed
— a direct legacy of the aducanumab controversy. Hands off rescheduling questions to the DEA-scheduling agent.
