# QUERIES — payer corpus

Six realistic checks the `payer` agent would run for dossier field **E4**, answered **from the corpus** (`index.jsonl`). Checks the corpus cannot answer are stated as the live-search gap.

### Q1. Will Medicare cover lecanemab broadly for Alzheimer's patients?
**A (from corpus):** Not unrestricted. CMS finalized NCD 200.3 in April 2022 requiring CED for all anti-amyloid mAbs. After lecanemab received traditional FDA approval in July 2023, CMS expanded coverage — but only when a physician participates in a qualifying registry ("coverage with evidence development"). Patients pay 20% Part B coinsurance. → Cards 3, 4.

### Q2. What is ICER's value-based price benchmark for lecanemab?
**A (from corpus):** ICER calculated a Health-Benefit Price Benchmark (HBPB) of **$8,900–$21,500/year** against lecanemab's $26,500/year list price. The independent panel voted 15-0 that the drug represents **"low" long-term value** at current pricing, and 12-3 that available evidence is "not adequate" to demonstrate net health benefit. → Card 1.

### Q3. Is there a precedent for ultra-rare CNS gene/RNA therapy drugs being deemed poor value at current prices?
**A (from corpus):** Yes. ICER assessed nusinersen (Spinraza, $750,000/year) as "low value" unanimously; value-based range was $72,000–$130,000/year. For Zolgensma (gene therapy, SMA), the cost-effective price threshold was estimated at $310,000–$900,000/treatment. → Card 2.

### Q4. What payer logic applies when a branded CNS addiction drug competes against generic alternatives?
**A (from corpus):** "Cost-minimization" logic — ICER found extended-release buprenorphine/naltrexone formulations provide only marginal incremental benefit over generics; Probuphine's list price needed a 53–65% reduction to meet cost-effectiveness thresholds. → Card 5.

### Q5. What coverage pathway applies to a CNS drug with FDA accelerated approval (surrogate endpoint) vs. traditional approval?
**A (from corpus):** Under NCD 200.3: accelerated approval (surrogate) → RCT-only coverage; traditional approval (direct clinical benefit) → broader CED in CMS-approved registries. The shift from restricted to broader Medicare coverage for lecanemab followed traditional approval in July 2023. → Cards 3, 4.
