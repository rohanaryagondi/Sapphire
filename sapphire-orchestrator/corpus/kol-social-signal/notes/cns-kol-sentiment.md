# CNS KOL sentiment — named experts on record (dossier F2)

What credentialed experts publicly say about a class is a forward-looking signal for Bucket 2. Two CNS
debates dominate the durable, attributable record; the **divergence** between optimists and skeptics is
itself the signal.

> **Scope note.** This corpus pre-ingests **named-expert-on-record** positions in durable, citable sources
> (journal editorials/viewpoints, society voices). The spec's tier scheme (T3 named-expert / T4 anonymous)
> is mapped to the corpus's T1/T2 gate as **T2**. **Ephemeral social signal (T4: X/Substack/LinkedIn/podcasts)
> is intentionally left to LIVE harvest** — it isn't reliably citable or stable enough to pre-ingest (see
> manifest gaps). Per the spec, the *validity* of any claim is checked against EMET, not asserted here.

## Anti-amyloid antibodies — predominantly skeptical KOL record
- **Knopman & Perlmutter (Neurology, 2021):** "Prescribing Aducanumab in the Face of Meager Efficacy and
  Real Risks." Knopman sat on the FDA advisory committee that reviewed aducanumab — a high-authority caution.
- **Bauchner & Alexander (Medical Care, 2022):** "Rejection of Aducanumab (Aduhelm) by the Health Care
  Community: Lessons Learned and the Path Ahead." Alexander was also an FDA AdComm member — signals
  community-level pushback, not just individual dissent.
- **Kurkinen (Adv Clin Exp Med, 2023):** lecanemab "is not the right drug" — "lecanemab did not slow
  cognitive decline in women… it enhanced the decline in study participants with 2 APOE4 genes." A pointed
  named-skeptic read of the subgroup data (validated against EMET below).

## Muscarinic antipsychotics (KarXT/Cobenfy) — cautiously optimistic KOL record
- **Javitt (Am J Psychiatry, 2025):** "Xanomeline-Trospium Treatment of Cognitive Impairments of
  Schizophrenia: Hope for Some, or Hope for All?" — a leading schizophrenia neuroscientist signalling
  measured enthusiasm about the first non-D2 mechanism, while flagging open questions on breadth.
- **Hasan & Abid (Cureus, 2024):** Cobenfy as "a significant advancement… a promising alternative,"
  especially for treatment-resistant patients. (Junior authors — down-weighted, included as a sentiment
  data-point.)

## The divergence read
The KOL record is **net-skeptical on anti-amyloid** (efficacy magnitude, subgroup signals, risk) and
**net-optimistic on the muscarinic class** (novel mechanism, dopamine-sparing). For a CNS program, this
maps to championing risk: an amyloid-adjacent asset inherits an actively critical KOL climate; a muscarinic
or novel-mechanism asset inherits a receptive one. **Validate any load-bearing KOL claim against EMET**
(the agent's explicit hand-off) before treating it as fact.
