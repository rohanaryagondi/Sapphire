# CNS reputational & institutional headwinds (dossier F4)

How a program's class, sponsor, and mechanism are *perceived* by press, institutions, and the scientific
establishment shapes FDA scrutiny, partner appetite, and recruitment — **independently of the science's
merit**. The amyloid field is the dominant CNS case; the agent's job is to surface the perception *and*
keep it distinct from validity.

## The amyloid field's credibility crisis (three compounding hits)
1. **Process scandal — Aduhelm.** A bipartisan congressional investigation (House Oversight + E&C, Dec 2022)
   found "the atypical FDA review process and corporate greed that preceded FDA's controversial decision to
   grant accelerated approval to Aduhelm." [oversight.house.gov] This stained both Biogen and FDA's
   accelerated-approval credibility.
2. **Outright fraud — Cassava/simufilam.** The SEC charged Cassava Sciences + former execs over misleading
   simufilam claims, with "a Cassava-affiliated university scientist also charged for manipulating clinical
   trial results"; >$40M paid (Sept 2024). [SEC 2024-151] A landmark data-integrity scandal.
3. **Foundational-paper retraction — Lesné.** Nature retracted the 2006 Aβ*56 paper (24 June 2024), a
   ~2,500-citation cornerstone, after image-manipulation findings — one of the most-cited retractions ever.
   [nature.com]

## Perception ≠ scientific merit (the agent's core discipline)
Despite those three hits, the **amyloid hypothesis itself remained independently supported**: all familial
early-onset AD mutations (APP/PSEN1/PSEN2) converge on amyloid-β — "46 (87%) [of mutation carriers] had all
three CSF biomarkers… amyloid β (Aβ)42 — in abnormal ranges." [Lanoiselée et al. 2017, PLoS Med — via EMET]
So the field carries a real **reputational headwind** (institutional skepticism, press scrutiny) that is
**separate** from whether the biology is right. A program must manage both — and the agent must never let the
reputational signal override T1–T2 evidence in the dossier.

## The read
- **Class headwind:** amyloid-adjacent CNS programs inherit elevated FDA scrutiny + press skepticism.
- **Sponsor/integrity headwind:** small-biotech Alzheimer's cognition claims now face heightened
  data-integrity skepticism (the Cassava effect).
- **Keep perception vs merit distinct** — and validate any load-bearing scientific claim via EMET.
