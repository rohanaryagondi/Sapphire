# CNS Guidances & Endpoint Precedent

What endpoints and trial designs the FDA *expects* in CNS indications — the standing rules a program is
measured against, distinct from one-off drug decisions. All **T1** (FDA primary; titles/dates/positions
confirmed via fetchable NIH/PMC/NCBI sources that reproduce the FDA text, canonical FDA/Federal Register
URLs retained) — see `manifest.md`.

**Alzheimer's — Early AD guidance (Feb 2018).** A stage-dependent endpoint framework: in presymptomatic
Stage 1 a biomarker effect can indicate treatment success, Stage 2 accepts neuropsychological-test
improvement, and only symptomatic stages require a functional co-primary
([FDA](https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/early-alzheimers-disease-developing-drugs-treatment); via [PMC6804505](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6804505/)).
How a sponsor *stages* its early-AD population dictates whether a biomarker endpoint is even admissible.

**ALS — final guidance (Sept 2019).** FDA's thinking on ALS trial design: earlier sponsor communication,
broader inclusion criteria, and a stated preference against placebo-only arms, scoped to ALS's core motor
aspects (weakness and survival) ([Federal Register 2019-20629](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2019/09/24/2019-20629/amyotrophic-lateral-sclerosis-developing-drugs-for-treatment-guidance-for-industry-availability)).

**MDD — draft guidance (June 2018).** The first major revision of the 1977 antidepressant guidelines,
covering monotherapy, combination, and adjunctive trial designs
([Federal Register 2018-13297](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2018/06/21/2018-13297/major-depressive-disorder-developing-drugs-for-treatment-draft-guidance-for-industry-availability)).
An MDD program's design is judged against this modern framework, not the legacy 1977 expectations.

**Epilepsy — partial-onset-seizure extrapolation (Sept 2019).** FDA concluded POS efficacy in adults can
be fully extrapolated to pediatric patients ≥1 month (requiring PK matching + long-term safety, not
independent pediatric efficacy trials) — but explicitly **not** for other seizure types
([Federal Register 2019-19291](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2019/09/06/2019-19291/drugs-for-treatment-of-partial-onset-seizures-full-extrapolation-of-efficacy-from-adults-to)).

**Migraine — acute-treatment guidance (Oct 2014).** Replaced four co-primary endpoints with two — pain
freedom and freedom from most-bothersome-symptom at 2 hours — reshaping how every modern acute-migraine
trial is powered ([FDA media/89829](https://www.fda.gov/media/89829/download); via [PubMed 30953576](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30953576/)).

**Pain — non-opioid analgesic guidances (acute draft Feb 2022).** Prefer patient-reported pain intensity
as the primary endpoint and warn that accelerated approval is hard to use because no surrogate reliably
predicts an analgesic effect ([FDA](https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/development-non-opioid-analgesics-acute-pain-draft-guidance-industry)).

**Rare neuro / DMD — guidance (Feb 2018).** Sets no required clinical outcome but accepts muscle/
dystrophin biomarkers as surrogate endpoints for accelerated approval "in some situations," with ~1 year
of safety exposure generally expected ([Federal Register 2018-03225](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2018/02/16/2018-03225/duchenne-muscular-dystrophy-and-related-dystrophinopathies-developing-drugs-for-treatment-guidance)).

**Cross-cutting — the "reasonably likely surrogate endpoint" standard (accelerated approval).** A
surrogate with strong mechanistic/epidemiologic support but not yet validated may support accelerated
approval, conditioned on a mandatory confirmatory trial ([FDA BEST, NCBI NBK453485](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK453485/)).
This is the framework under every CNS biomarker approval (amyloid, NfL, dystrophin) — see
`endpoint-precedent.md`.
