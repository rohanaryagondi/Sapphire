# CNS Advisory Committee (AdComm) Precedent

The votes, and what they turned on. **Critical interpretive rule for the agent:** an AdComm outcome is
*advisory, not binding*. A negative vote is a strong **`precedent`** signal but is **not** itself a
dispositive veto — the dispositive FDA action is the agency's own CRL/withdrawal. FDA has overridden a
unanimous "no" (aducanumab) and a positive vote does not guarantee approval. All cards **T2** (FDA.gov
AdComm pages blocked WebFetch; anchored to sponsor/secondary records that quote the vote) — see `manifest.md`.

**Aducanumab (Biogen, AD) — 0-10-1 against, Nov 2020.** The PCNS Drugs AdComm voted 0 yes / 10 no / 1
uncertain that Study 302 could be considered primary evidence of effectiveness, citing the conflict
between the two pivotal trials ([PMC11694499](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11694499/)). FDA
granted accelerated approval anyway in June 2021, prompting committee resignations. The discriminator for
amyloid-targeting AD programs is a convincing, *non-discordant* pivotal trial.

**Lecanemab (Eisai/Biogen, early AD) — unanimous yes, June 2023.** The PCNS AdComm voted unanimously that
the Phase 3 CLARITY AD data confirmed clinical benefit, supporting traditional approval that July
([BioArctic, 2023-06](https://www.bioarctic.com/en/fda-advisory-committee-votes-unanimously-to-confirm-clinical-benefit-of-leqembi-lecanemab-irmb-for-the-treatment-of-early-alzheimers-disease/)).
The instructive contrast to aducanumab: a single clean confirmatory trial flipped the amyloid hypothesis
from debacle to clean endorsement — the target class was never the problem; the trial discordance was.

**Tofersen (Biogen, SOD1-ALS) — 9-0 on the biomarker, 5-3 on direct efficacy, Mar 2023.** The committee
voted 9-0 that plasma NfL reduction is "reasonably likely" to predict clinical benefit, while splitting
5-3 on direct clinical efficacy ([Biogen, 2023](https://investors.biogen.com/news-releases/news-release-details/fda-grants-accelerated-approval-qalsodytm-tofersen-sod1-als)).
The first FDA-endorsed CNS biomarker surrogate, underpinning accelerated approval (Apr 2023).

**AMX0035 / Relyvrio (Amylyx, ALS) — 4-6 then 7-2, the rare same-committee reversal.** A "no" (4 yes /
6 no, Mar 2022) became a "yes" (7 yes / 2 no, Sept 2022) after new analyses of the *existing* CENTAUR
trial ([Amylyx, 2022-09](https://www.amylyx.com/news/amylyx-pharmaceuticals-announces-fda-advisory-committee-supports-approval-of-amx0035-for-the-treatment-of-als)).
The drug's later 2024 withdrawal (failed PHOENIX confirmatory) is the cautionary tail on single-trial
approvals under patient pressure.

**Esketamine / Spravato (Janssen, TRD) — 14-2-1 in favor, Feb 2019.** A joint Psychopharmacologic +
Drug Safety AdComm endorsed benefit-risk, paving a REMS-gated approval for a Schedule III agent in
treatment-resistant depression ([Pharmacy Times, 2019](https://www.pharmacytimes.com/view/fda-advisory-committee-recommends-approval-of-nasal-spray-for-treatmentresistant-depression)).
Precedent: FDA will approve an abuse-liability CNS agent when benefit-risk and a risk-mitigation program
are accepted.

**MDMA-assisted therapy (Lykos, PTSD) — 2-9 efficacy, 1-10 benefit-risk, June 2024.** Voted down on both
questions; the committee fixated on functional unblinding, expectation bias, and abuse-AE documentation
gaps ([HCPLive, 2024-06](https://www.hcplive.com/view/live-updates-fda-psychopharmacologic-advisory-committee-meeting-mdma-ptsd)).
The reusable lesson: **functional unblinding in a strongly psychoactive CNS trial is a documented AdComm
dealbreaker.**
