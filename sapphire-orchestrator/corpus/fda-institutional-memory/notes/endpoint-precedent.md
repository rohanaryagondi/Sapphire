# CNS Endpoint Precedent — Surrogate Approvals, Confirmatory Outcomes & Clinical Holds

How the FDA has actually *applied* its surrogate-endpoint and safety-hold powers to specific CNS /
neuromuscular programs. Read alongside `guidances.md` (the standing rules) — this note is the case law.
Tiers per `manifest.md` (eteplirsen verified via a primary FDA announcement → **T1**; the rest **T2**).

## Accelerated approval on a surrogate — and what came next

FDA repeatedly accepts surrogate biomarkers for accelerated approval in serious CNS/neuromuscular disease,
but the record shows a clear pattern about the confirmatory step.

- **Aducanumab (Aduhelm, Biogen) — AD, accelerated approval June 2021** on the amyloid-beta-plaque
  surrogate, over a near-unanimous AdComm "no," and later discontinued
  ([Drugs.com FDA history](https://www.drugs.com/history/aduhelm.html)). Amyloid-surrogate-*only* AD
  programs are now a high-scrutiny precedent.
- **Lecanemab (Leqembi, Eisai/Biogen) — AD, accelerated Jan 2023 → traditional approval July 2023** once
  CLARITY AD demonstrated slowed clinical decline ([Eisai, 2023](https://www.eisai.com/news/2023/news202349.html)).
  This is the surrogate-to-clinical conversion aducanumab never achieved — the template FDA now expects.
- **Tofersen (Qalsody, Biogen/Ionis) — SOD1-ALS, accelerated approval Apr 2023** on plasma **NfL**
  reduction even though the VALOR primary clinical endpoint missed; ATLAS is the confirmatory trial
  ([Oligonucleotide Therapeutics Society](https://oligotherapeutics.org/an-aso-is-granted-accelerated-approval-by-the-fda/)).
  The reference precedent for a biomarker-led ALS/neurodegeneration program.
- **Eteplirsen (Exondys 51, Sarepta) — DMD, accelerated approval Sept 2016** on increased skeletal-muscle
  dystrophin, explicitly without established clinical benefit, over internal/AdComm skepticism — the
  landmark, much-debated low-evidence approval ([FDA via PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/fda-grants-accelerated-approval-to-first-drug-for-duchenne-muscular-dystrophy-300330093.html)). **(T1.)**
- **Nusinersen (Spinraza, Biogen/Ionis) — SMA, standard approval Dec 2016** on a real clinical endpoint
  (motor-milestone response, 40% vs 0% in ENDEAR) ([PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/spinraza-nusinersen-approved-in-us-to-treat-broad-range-of-patients-with-spinal-muscular-atrophy-300383512.html)).
  The contrast case: FDA prefers a clinical readout where one is achievable, and this is the bar
  surrogate-based CNS programs are measured against.

## Clinical holds — a single safety event stops a program

- **Pfizer mini-dystrophin gene therapy (PF-06939926) — DMD, clinical hold Dec 2021** after a patient
  death in a Phase 1b study (preceded by muscle-weakness/myocarditis SAEs); later lifted
  ([pharmaphorum](https://pharmaphorum.com/news/pfizer-pauses-duchenne-gene-therapy-trial-after-patient-death)).
  A single trial death triggers an FDA hold on a neuromuscular gene-therapy IND.
- **Sarepta AAVrh74 platform — DMD/LGMD, clinical holds + market-suspension request + platform-designation
  revocation, July 2025** after three deaths from acute liver failure across platform products, reaching
  the already-approved Elevidys ([CGTLive](https://www.cgtlive.com/view/fda-revokes-sarepta-platform-technology-designation-muscular-dystrophy-gene-therapy-vector-following-patient-deaths)).
  Strong recent precedent: a shared mechanistic liability (hepatotoxicity) can gate an entire AAV
  platform and reach an approved product — a class/platform veto, not just one program.
