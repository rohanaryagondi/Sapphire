# CNS Complete Response Letters (CRLs) & Refuse-to-File

What the FDA *declined to approve* in CNS, and the documented reason. The agent uses these to test a
flaw hypothesis of the form "has the FDA rejected a program for the reason mine might be rejected?"
Each is **T2** (anchored to the sponsor's own announcement of the FDA action) unless noted — see `manifest.md`.

**Pimavanserin in dementia-related psychosis (Acadia, CRL April 2021).** Despite a positive pivotal
HARMONY trial, FDA issued a CRL citing "lack of statistical significance in some of the subgroups of
dementia, and insufficient numbers of patients with certain less common dementia subtypes" as a failure
of substantial evidence ([Acadia press release, 2021-04](https://acadia.com/en-us/media/news-releases/acadia-pharmaceuticals-receives-complete-response-letter-us-fda)).
Precedent: for a broad, heterogeneous CNS label a single positive trial can be judged insufficient when
the agency expects efficacy to hold across subgroups.

**NurOwn in ALS (BrainStorm, Refuse-to-File November 2022).** FDA refused to file the BLA for the
autologous cell therapy; the pivotal Phase 3 had missed its primary endpoint and the data did not cross
the substantial-evidence threshold ([BrainStorm/PRNewswire, 2022-11](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/brainstorm-cell-therapeutics-receives-refusal-to-file-letter-from-fda-for-its-new-biologics-license-application-for-nurown-for-the-treatment-of-als-301673753.html)).
Precedent: a missed primary endpoint can stop a CNS program at the *filing* gate, before substantive
review, even under intense patient-advocacy pressure.

**Zuranolone in MDD (Biogen/Sage, CRL August 2023).** On the same day FDA approved zuranolone for
postpartum depression, it issued a CRL for the larger MDD indication, finding the evidence "insufficient
proof that the medicine...is effective for MDD" ([BioPharma Dive, 2023-08](https://www.biopharmadive.com/news/sage-biogen-antidepressant-zuranolone-fda-approval/689997/)).
Precedent: the same molecule can clear a narrow indication and be rejected for the broad one — indication
breadth must be matched by indication-specific substantial evidence.

**MDMA-assisted therapy for PTSD (Lykos, CRL August 2024).** Following an advisory committee that voted
2-9 against efficacy and 1-10 against benefit-risk (June 2024), FDA issued a CRL and requested an
additional Phase 3 ([Lykos/PRNewswire, 2024-08](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/lykos-therapeutics-announces-complete-response-letter-for-midomafetamine-capsules-for-ptsd-302219182.html)).
Concerns centered on separating drug effect from psychotherapy, functional unblinding, and abuse-related
AE documentation. Precedent: for a novel CNS/psychedelic modality entangled with psychotherapy, FDA
demands a fresh adequate-and-well-controlled trial, and an adverse AdComm reliably foreshadows the CRL.

**AXS-07 for acute migraine (Axsome, CRL May 2022).** FDA rejected AXS-07 on chemistry-manufacturing-
controls (CMC) grounds alone; per Axsome the "CRL did not identify or raise any concerns about the
clinical efficacy or safety data...and the FDA did not request any new clinical trials"
([Axsome/PRNewswire, 2022-05](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/axsome-therapeutics-receives-fda-complete-response-letter-for-new-drug-application-for-axs-07-for-the-acute-treatment-of-migraine-301537093.html)).
Precedent: a clean clinical dataset is not sufficient — CMC readiness is an independent gate that can
trigger a CRL.
