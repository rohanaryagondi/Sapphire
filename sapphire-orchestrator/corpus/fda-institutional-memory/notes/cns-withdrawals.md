# CNS Withdrawals, Market Actions & Major Safety/Boxed-Warning Communications

Drugs pulled or restricted *post-approval*, and the liability that drove it. These are the strongest
veto-candidate precedents: a CNS drug already off the market for a liability a new program shares is the
clearest "the FDA has ruled on this flaw before" signal. Tiers per `manifest.md` (pemoline + zolpidem
verified against a directly-fetched primary record → **T1**; the rest **T2**).

**Lorcaserin (Belviq) — withdrawal, Feb 2020.** FDA requested Eisai withdraw the serotonergic
weight-management drug after a postmarketing randomized safety trial showed an increased occurrence of
cancer (pancreatic, colorectal, lung), the imbalance widening over longer treatment
([FDA DSC, 2020-02](https://www.fda.gov/drugs/drug-safety-communications/fda-requests-withdrawal-weight-loss-drug-belviq-belviq-xr-lorcaserin-market)).
Precedent: a long-duration carcinogenicity signal emerging years after approval can force removal of a
chronic CNS-active agent.

**Pergolide (Permax) — withdrawal, Mar 2007.** The ergot-derived dopamine agonist for Parkinson's was
withdrawn at FDA's request over cardiac valvulopathy, after a 2003 label warning and a 2006 boxed warning,
with NEJM studies (Jan 2007) quantifying a several-fold valve-damage risk
([NBC News, 2007](https://www.nbcnews.com/health/health-news/fda-pulls-parkinson-s-drug-market-flna1c9475114)).
Precedent: the canonical **5-HT2B-mediated valvular-fibrosis** withdrawal for dopaminergic/serotonergic
CNS drugs — an off-target receptor liability.

**Pemoline (Cylert) — withdrawal, marketing ceased Oct 2005.** FDA concluded the ADHD stimulant's
liver-toxicity risk (hepatic failure leading to transplant or death) outweighed its benefits; approvals
were formally withdrawn in later Federal Register notices
([govinfo.gov, FR-2023-05-23](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/FR-2023-05-23/pdf/2023-10924.pdf)).
Precedent: cumulative idiosyncratic hepatotoxicity is dispositive for a CNS drug whose benefit is not
life-saving. **(T1 — primary FR record fetched.)**

**Valdecoxib (Bextra) — withdrawal, Apr 2005.** Pfizer withdrew the COX-2 analgesic at FDA's
recommendation for cardiovascular events (notably in CABG trials), inadequate long-term CV data, and
serious skin reactions including Stevens-Johnson syndrome
([valdecoxib record](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valdecoxib), corroborated by FDA review memo Apr 2005).
Precedent for pain/CNS-adjacent programs: an unfavorable benefit-risk versus existing therapy combined
with a serious-cutaneous signal is withdrawal-grade.

**Pediatric antidepressant suicidality — boxed warning, Oct 2004.** FDA directed a class-wide boxed
warning after a pooled analysis of 24 short-term pediatric trials (>4,400 patients, nine drugs) showed
suicidality ~4% on drug versus ~2% on placebo, with no completed suicides
([FDA postmarket safety page](https://www.fda.gov/drugs/postmarket-drug-safety-information-patients-and-providers/suicidality-children-and-adolescents-being-treated-antidepressant-medications)).
Precedent: any new pediatric CNS antidepressant must anticipate the pooled-suicidality signal in design
and labeling.

**Antipsychotics in elderly dementia — boxed warning, 2005 then class-wide 2008.** The 2005 atypical-
antipsychotic mortality warning (~1.6-1.7-fold increased death) was extended to all antipsychotics in
June 2008 ([Psychiatric News, 2008](https://psychiatryonline.org/doi/10.1176/pn.43.14.0001)).
Precedent: a dementia-agitation/psychosis indication must confront **all-cause mortality** directly, not
only behavioral efficacy.

**Zolpidem dosing — safety action, Jan 2013.** FDA required lower recommended doses (women's IR 10→5 mg)
because next-morning blood levels can impair driving and women clear the drug more slowly (Cmax/AUC ~45%
higher) ([PMC4560192](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4560192/)). Precedent: **PK sex
differences** and next-morning functional-impairment endpoints drive FDA dosing mandates for CNS sedatives.
**(T1 — primary regulatory action read via PMC.)**

**Opioid + benzodiazepine combination — boxed warning, Aug 2016.** FDA imposed class-wide boxed warnings
across ~400 opioid and benzodiazepine products for respiratory depression, coma, and death from combined
use ([FDA announcement, 2016-08](https://www.fda.gov/drugs/information-drug-class/new-safety-measures-announced-opioid-analgesics-prescription-opioid-cough-products-and)).
Precedent: any CNS-depressant program must address additive respiratory/CNS-depression risk in combination.

**Varenicline (Chantix) — boxed-warning REMOVAL, Dec 2016.** After the FDA-co-designed EAGLES trial found
neuropsychiatric risk lower than suspected, FDA removed the neuropsychiatric boxed warning
([Pfizer release, 2016-12](https://www.pfizer.com/news/press-release/press-release-detail/fda_approves_removal_of_boxed_warning_regarding_serious_neuropsychiatric_events_from_chantix_varenicline)).
Precedent (the optimistic one): a boxed warning is *reversible* with a sufficiently powered safety trial
in the at-risk population — useful when arguing a program can de-risk a psychiatric-AE label.
