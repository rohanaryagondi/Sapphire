# SMA franchise as a CNS freedom-to-operate (FTO) exemplar

The spinal muscular atrophy (SMA) franchise is the cleanest teaching case for CNS FTO because the **same disease (SMN restoration) is addressed by all three modalities Quiver cares about** — an antisense oligonucleotide (nusinersen), a small molecule (risdiplam), and an AAV9 gene therapy (onasemnogene abeparvovec / Zolgensma). The patent landscape therefore exposes, in one place, the composition-of-matter, method-of-use, and platform/modality IP a program must clear for each route. All facts below were read from the granted-patent records on Google Patents (USPTO/WIPO), retrieved 2026-06-25; each quote is a verbatim substring (abstract or independent claim) of the cited patent.

## ASO route — SMN2 splicing (nusinersen / Spinraza)
Two Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory / Ionis (now also Biogen MA) patents anchor the SMN2-splicing ASO route:
- **[US 8,361,977 B2](https://patents.google.com/patent/US8361977B2/en)** (priority 2005-06-23; **active, ~2030-12-23**) — compositions + methods for modulating SMN2 mRNA splicing. The foundational SMN2-splicing family.
- **[US 8,980,853 B2](https://patents.google.com/patent/US8980853B2/en)** (priority 2009-06-17; **active, ~2030-11-24**) — the method of administering an ASO **complementary to SMN2 intron 7 (ISS-N1) into the cerebrospinal fluid** (the precise nusinersen mechanism).

**FTO read:** an ASO that boosts SMN2 exon-7 inclusion via the intron-7/ISS-N1 region delivered intrathecally is a **veto-candidate** against this family until ~2030.

## Small-molecule route — SMN2 splicing modifier (risdiplam / Evrysdi)
- **[US 9,969,754 B2](https://patents.google.com/patent/US9969754B2/en)** (Roche / PTC Therapeutics; priority 2014-05-15; **active, ~2035-05-11**) — the granted US composition-of-matter member of the risdiplam formula-(I) genus; the family contested in the Roche v. Natco risdiplam litigation.

**FTO read:** any small-molecule SMN2 exon-7 splicing modifier in the risdiplam chemical genus is a **veto-candidate** through ~2035 — the longest-lived blocker in the franchise.

## Gene-therapy route — AAV9-delivered SMN (Zolgensma and around it)
- **[US 10,821,154 B2](https://patents.google.com/patent/US10821154B2/en)** (Genzyme / Sanofi; priority 2013-05-01; **active, ~2033-07-05**) — a method of treating SMA in a primate by administering an **AAV9 rAAV encoding SMN to the spinal cord / cisterna magna**. This is the **live AAV9-SMA blocker**, and notably it is held by a third party (Genzyme), *not* the Zolgensma sponsor.
- **[WO 2019/094253 A1](https://patents.google.com/patent/WO2019094253A1/en)** (AveXis / Novartis Gene Therapies; priority 2017-11-08; **ceased PCT**) — the Zolgensma-family AAV9-SMN preparation/purification disclosure. As a ceased PCT it is freedom-relevant prior-art/landscape, **not** an enforceable US blocker.
- **[US 7,906,111 B2](https://patents.google.com/patent/US7906111B2/en)** (Univ. of Pennsylvania / Wilson; priority 2003-09-30; **lapsed, ~2026-01-16**) — the foundational AAV9 capsid/clade composition patent (the capsid Zolgensma uses). As it expires in **Jan 2026** the AAV9 capsid itself becomes a freedom point rather than a blocker.

**FTO read:** the *capsid* (AAV9) is opening up (UPenn expiry 2026), but the *AAV9-SMN-to-CNS method* is still blocked by Genzyme (US10821154) to ~2033 — a reminder that capsid freedom ≠ indication freedom.

## Platform chemistry — the ASO backbone
- **[US 9,550,988 B2](https://patents.google.com/patent/US9550988B2/en)** (Ionis; priority 2006-10-18; **active, ~2028-11-13**) — gapmer ASOs combining bicyclic + non-bicyclic high-affinity wings on a phosphorothioate backbone, claimed for **reduced toxicity** (the 2'-MOE/2nd-generation platform).

**FTO read:** a CNS ASO using mixed-chemistry gapmers within these claims must clear Ionis platform IP through ~2028 — relevant to *any* ASO program, not just SMA.

## Expiry cliffs (composite FTO timeline)
UPenn AAV9 capsid **2026** → Ionis gapmer platform **2028** → nusinersen SMN2 family **2030** → Genzyme AAV9-SMA method **2033** → risdiplam composition **2035**. A program's earliest clean entry depends on which modality + claim surface it practices.

> **Scope note.** This corpus is anchored on the SMA franchise as the cross-modality exemplar; FTO for other CNS targets/indications, Orange/Purple Book exclusivity dates, and full PTAB/Paragraph-IV litigation records are live-search gaps (see `manifest.md`).
