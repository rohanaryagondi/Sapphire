# CNS consolidation at scale + the clinical-failure tape

Two further patterns the investor record encodes: big-pharma buying **de-risked** CNS revenue, and the
high base-rate of CNS clinical failure that resets valuations overnight.

## J&J → Intra-Cellular ($14.6B, Jan 2025) — paying up for an approved franchise
"Johnson & Johnson will acquire all outstanding shares of Intra-Cellular Therapies… for $132.00 per share
in cash for a total equity value of approximately $14.6 billion." [SEC 8-K, Intra-Cellular, Ex-99.1] The
asset is **CAPLYTA (lumateperone)** — already FDA-approved for bipolar depression and schizophrenia, with
a pending MDD label-expansion sNDA and "$5 billion+ potential in peak year sales." Unlike the pre-approval
Karuna/Cerevel bets, this is a **commercial-stage, lower-PoS-risk** purchase — the premium buys de-risked
revenue and label-expansion upside, not a binary trial outcome.

## Neumora navacaprant — the failure tape (Jan 2025)
"Navacaprant did not achieve primary or secondary endpoints in the KOASTAL-1 phase 3 trial for MDD. Both
navacaprant and placebo showed similar improvements in depression scores, with no significant difference."
[Psychiatric Times, reporting Neumora's KOASTAL-1 results — the primary endpoint was change in MADRS at
Week 6]. navacaprant is a kappa-opioid receptor (KOR) antagonist; shares fell ~80% (per press), and the
full KOASTAL program was later discontinued with a ~35% workforce cut.

## The read
- **Stage de-risks price:** an approved franchise (Caplyta) commands a large but lower-variance valuation;
  a pre-approval mechanism bet (Karuna/Cerevel) is binary.
- **MDD/CNS is a graveyard:** a novel mechanism (KOR antagonism) can erase most of a company's value in one
  readout — the financing/runway consequences (restructuring, layoffs) follow directly from the clinical miss.
- For the **E2 competitive-pipeline** dossier field, the investor record (deal price, deal structure, and
  the failure tape) is a fast, candid read on where the field actually prices each program's PoS.
