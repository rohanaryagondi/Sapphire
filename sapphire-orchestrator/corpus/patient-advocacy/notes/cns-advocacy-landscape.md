# CNS patient-advocacy landscape (dossier F1)

Organized patient communities shape FDA benefit-risk framing, drive recruitment, and determine whether an
approved drug actually reaches patients. The CNS field has several of the most organized communities in
medicine; their leverage points differ.

## Advocacy shaping ACCESS — Alzheimer's Association vs the CMS coverage gate
After CMS restricted Medicare coverage of anti-amyloid antibodies to registry-based "coverage with evidence
development" (2022), the Alzheimer's Association ran a broad campaign to broaden access: "following extensive
efforts by the Alzheimer's Association, bipartisan members of Congress, state attorneys general, clinicians
and, most importantly, advocates from communities all across the country, CMS has [revised its approach]."
[alz.org] **Note the divergence:** advocacy pushed *for* access while the named-KOL record (corpus #4) and
HTA bodies (corpus #1/#3) were skeptical of the drugs' benefit — a real tension a program inherits.

## Advocacy shaping the FDA FRAMEWORK — the ALS community
ALS is one of the most organized patient communities. FDA issued ALS-specific drug-development guidance
(Sept 2019) — "to assist sponsors in the clinical development of drugs and biological products for the
treatment of amyotrophic lateral sclerosis" [fda.gov] — a tailored, patient-focused benefit-risk/endpoint
framework the community pressed for. The urgency is quantified: ALS "patients typically die from respiratory
failure within 3 years of symptom onset," and riluzole "extends the average survival of patients by 3-6
months" [Hardiman et al. 2011, Nat Rev Neurol — via EMET]. That unmet need is the engine of ALS advocacy.

## Advocacy shaping an FDA DECISION — DMD / eteplirsen
Eteplirsen's 2016 accelerated approval — "the first oligonucleotide to be approved based on very limited
data," its approval "shrouded in controversy" [PMC commentary] — is the textbook case where intense DMD
patient/family advocacy is widely credited with influencing the FDA over internal staff objections. (Ties
to the eteplirsen regulatory-divergence card in corpus #1.)

## Scale & funding of organized advocacy — and the conflict it can create
The Michael J. Fox Foundation reports deploying tens of millions (e.g. "$62.4M for Tools to Track PD and
Therapies") [michaeljfox.org] — a marker of how well-funded CNS advocacy sets research agendas. **Funding can
color positions:** the Alzheimer's Association's top corporate donors include the makers of anti-amyloid
antibodies (Biogen, Eisai, Lilly, Genentech), which critics tie to its pro-access stance — a conflict the
agent should surface (the org states donations don't influence its positions). This funding-ties analysis is
best done **live** off current IRS 990s (see manifest gaps).
