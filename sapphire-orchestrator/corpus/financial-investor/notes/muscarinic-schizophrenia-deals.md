# The muscarinic-schizophrenia M&A wave — deal value vs realized PoS

Deal structures encode how acquirers privately priced probability of success. The 2023–24 muscarinic
wave is the cleanest recent CNS example — two near-simultaneous multi-billion-dollar bets on the *same*
mechanism, with opposite outcomes.

## The thesis (biomedical grounding)
M1/M4 muscarinic agonism reduces psychosis **without** dopamine D2 blockade — validated in Phase 2
EMERGENT-1: KarXT (xanomeline-trospium) cut PANSS total by 11.6 points more than placebo
("−17.4 points with xanomeline-trospium and −5.9 points with placebo… −11.6 points; 95% CI −16.1 to −7.1;
P<0.001"). [Brannan et al., *NEJM* 2021, PMID 33626254 — via EMET; trial funded by Karuna Therapeutics]

## BMS → Karuna ($14.0B, Dec 2023) — the bet that paid off
"Bristol Myers Squibb has agreed to acquire Karuna for $330.00 per share in cash, for a total equity value
of $14.0 billion, or $12.7 billion net of estimated cash acquired." [SEC 8-K, BMS, Ex-99.1] An all-cash
buyout of a **pre-approval** asset (KarXT) implies high PoS; vindicated when KarXT (Cobenfy) won FDA
approval in Sep 2024.

## AbbVie → Cerevel ($8.7B, Dec 2023) — the bet that broke
"Transaction valued at $45.00 per share in cash, for a total equity value of approximately $8.7 billion."
[SEC 8-K, Cerevel, Ex-99.1] Centered on **emraclidine**, an M4 positive allosteric modulator — same
muscarinic thesis, different molecule. Then in Nov 2024 emraclidine "did not meet their primary endpoint…
in the Positive and Negative Syndrome Scale (PANSS) total score compared to the placebo group at week 6"
in both EMPOWER-1/-2 Phase 2 trials. [AbbVie PR] AbbVie took a large impairment (reported ~$3.5B; AbbVie's
SEC filing not fetched this pass).

## The read
Same mechanism, two prices, two outcomes: a *clinically validated mechanism* (KarXT) does not guarantee a
*second molecule* against the same target (emraclidine) will work. Deal value concentrated in one asset on
one mechanism is fragile — a single Phase 2 readout can erase most of the premise. The contrast is the
core "implied-PoS vs realized-risk" lesson for the competitive-pipeline (E2) dossier field.
