# Dr. Robin Sherrington, PhD
EVP Strategy & Innovation, Xenon Pharmaceuticals
## 1. Role & Scope
As EVP Strategy & Innovation at Xenon Pharmaceuticals, Dr. Sherrington oversees the scientific direction and strategic positioning of the company's ion channel-focused neurology pipeline. With over 20 years in neuroscience drug development, she bridges academic rigor with commercial realities, managing a portfolio of phase 3 programs targeting high-burden neurological conditions. Her role encompasses platform stewardship, competitive landscaping, clinical trial strategy, and early-stage innovation pipeline expansion. She reports directly to CEO Ian Mortimer and serves as the primary scientific voice to investors, regulatory agencies, and academic collaborators.
Key Responsibilities:
- Clinical trial design and regulatory strategy for Kv7 and Kv channel programs
- Portfolio optimization and candidate prioritization across epilepsy, MDD, and pain indications
- Competitive positioning versus other ion channel and epilepsy programs
- Long-term open-label extension (OLE) data analysis and patient registry management
## 2. Strategic Priorities
Dr. Sherrington is acutely focused on advancing azetukalner (XEN1101) as the first-in-class Kv7 potassium channel opener for focal seizures and comorbid major depressive disorder. The 48-month OLE data represents a critical dataset for sustained safety and efficacy, positioning Xenon for differentiated labeling. She is equally invested in expanding the Kv7 and broader Kv channel platform, ensuring the company captures first-mover advantage before competitors enter the space. Her strategic lens includes ecosystem partnerships, real-world evidence collection, and post-launch commercialization infrastructure.
Immediate Objectives:
- Accelerate Phase 3 recruitment for focal seizure and MDD cohorts
- Analyze 48-month OLE extension data for long-term safety signals and durability claims
- Finalize Phase 2b/3 development plans for XEN1701 and XEN1120 pain programs
- Establish advisory boards and KOL engagement strategy for refractory epilepsy community
## 3. Scientific Expertise & Background
Dr. Sherrington holds a PhD in Molecular Neurobiology from UC San Diego and completed postdoctoral training in ion channel electrophysiology at the Max Planck Institute. She has published over 80 peer-reviewed papers on voltage-gated potassium channels, neuronal excitability, and seizure mechanisms. Her previous role as SVP Clinical Development at a mid-stage CNS biotech exposed her to end-to-end drug development, regulatory submission strategy, and FDA interactions. She is intimately familiar with the Kv7 mechanism of action, having followed the academic literature since the inception of retigabine and seeing first-hand the gap left after its withdrawal.
Core Competencies:
- Deep expertise in Kv7 and ion channel biology across CNS and pain pathways
- Clinical trial design and operational excellence in refractory epilepsy populations
- Regulatory strategy and FDA pathways for neurological indications
- Long-term safety pharmacovigilance and real-world outcomes analysis
## 4. Key Challenges & Pain Points
As a first-in-class program, azetukalner faces several critical hurdles. Patient recruitment in focal seizure trials remains slower than industry benchmarks, compounded by the niche nature of refractory epilepsy populations. Competitive intensity from other Kv7 programs and broader antiseizure drug launches threatens market share assumptions. The dual indication strategy (focal seizures + MDD) presents complexity in trial design and label optimization. Long-term safety expectations continue to rise post-Vraylar and other CNS launches; any durability or emergence of novel safety signals could derail the program. Investor confidence hinges on clear competitive differentiation, which requires robust OLE data analysis.
Critical Pressure Points:
- Patient recruitment timelines and site activation in focal seizure trials
- Competitive threats from emerging Kv7 rivals and other ion channel programs
- Long-term safety profile interpretation and differentiation from retigabine
- Dual indication complexity and label negotiation with regulatory authorities

## 6. Market Understanding & Industry Perspective
Dr. Sherrington views the neurology biotech landscape as increasingly crowded yet fragmented. Focal epilepsy remains underserved despite the existence of over 20 approved ASDs; many patients are still refractory or intolerant. The MDD comorbidity in epilepsy is well-documented but rarely addressed as a dual indication—Xenon's approach is differentiated. The broader ion channel space is enjoying renewed investor interest post-GLP-1 fatigue, though CNS programs remain de-risked compared to metabolic targets. She anticipates consolidation among mid-stage epilepsy biotech, positioning Xenon as an attractive acquisition target or partnership opportunity.
Market Observations:
- Focal seizure market remains underpenetrated despite multiple ASD options
- Investor appetite for ion channel programs is rebounding; differentiators are critical
- MDD + comorbid condition strategies are attractive to payers and pharma partners
- Consolidation pressure may accelerate post-Phase 3 readout; partnership optionality is key
