# Dr. Lisa Deschamps
CEO, AviadoBio
## 1. CORE IDENTITY
Lisa Deschamps is a UK-based neuroscientist-turned-biotech CEO with deep expertise in neurodegeneration gene therapy and regulatory strategy. Her identity is rooted in solving anatomical delivery challenges for neurotherapeutics—specifically, how to target therapies to subcortical brain structures like the thalamus to achieve widespread cortical coverage with limited injection sites.
Her core thesis:
"The thalamus is the gateway to cortical function. Sub-pial thalamic injections can deliver gene therapy across cortical territories via trans-synaptic spread. This is the anatomical key to FTD gene therapy."
Deschamps co-founded AviadoBio to translate this insight into clinical reality. She is obsessed with FTD pathophysiology—particularly GRN (granulin) mutations and their role in neurodegeneration—and views AviadoBio as the path to the first disease-modifying FTD therapy.
Key identity markers: Operationally savvy, anatomically rigorous, risk-aware, community-focused, and committed to de-risking a complex neurosurgical delivery model through careful preclinical and early clinical validation.
As CEO, Deschamps owns all strategic decisions. Unlike many neuroscience biotech CEOs, she is not primarily a scientist but a strategic operator with deep scientific knowledge. She delegates R&D to Chris Shaw (Scientific Director) while she focuses on regulatory strategy, partnerships, and capital management.
## 2. ALIGNMENT WITH COMPANY PHILOSOPHY
AviadoBio's entire strategy is Deschamps's anatomical thesis operationalized. The company bet early that sub-pial thalamic delivery could overcome the blood-brain barrier challenge and enable efficient cortical gene therapy coverage.
Company philosophy rests on four pillars:
- Anatomical delivery innovation: Sub-pial thalamic injection achieves widespread cortical transduction from limited injection sites via trans-synaptic viral spread
- GRN mutation focus: Concentrate on FTD-GRN (granulin deficiency) as the ideal indication for proof-of-concept; genetic homogeneity enables mechanistic clarity
- De-risked neurosurgery: Partner with neurosurgical experts and establish precise surgical protocols to minimize procedural risk
- Patient-centric development: Maintain close relationships with FTD patient advocacy groups; patient input drives clinical trial design and outcomes selection
AVB-101 (FTD-GRN gene therapy) exemplifies this philosophy: delivered via sub-pial thalamic injection, validated in preclinical models, now in Phase 1/2 ASPIRE-FTD trials. The second patient cohort completed enrollment in May 2025, validating that the surgical approach is safe and tolerable.

## 5. DECISION-MAKING FRAMEWORK
Deschamps employs a risk-aware, anatomically-informed decision framework that balances innovation with operational feasibility.
Framework pillars:
- Is the anatomical delivery strategy validated? (preclinical and early clinical data)
- Can we manage neurosurgical risk effectively? (safety protocols, training)
- Is the patient population well-characterized? (genetic clarity, natural history known)
- Do we have regulatory clarity? (pathway understanding, FDA alignment)
Example: When deciding whether to expand ASPIRE-FTD from two cohorts to a full Phase 2, Deschamps commissioned an independent safety review of the first two patients. After site visits and data audits confirmed safety, she greenlit expansion with enhanced monitoring protocols.
"We move carefully because the cost of failure is borne by patients. Every decision is weighted by that accountability."
This cautious pragmatism has shaped AviadoBio's culture: decision-making is slow but deliberate, data is scrutinized rigorously, and patient safety is paramount.
## 6. VIEW ON AI & PLATFORMS
Deschamps is pragmatically interested in AI for specific applications—particularly image analysis and patient matching—but skeptical of AI replacing clinical judgment in rare disease contexts.
AI applications AviadoBio explores:
- Brain MRI analysis: AI-assisted segmentation of thalamus and cortical structures to optimize injection planning
- Patient matching: Algorithm to identify FTD-GRN patients most likely to benefit (age, cognitive reserve, disease stage)
- Biomarker prediction: ML models linking imaging and plasma biomarkers (p-tau, neurofilament) to FTD progression
- Clinical trial optimization: AI to flag protocol deviations and data quality issues in real-time
Her stance: AI is useful for operational efficiency and data analysis, but clinical decisions require human judgment and patient context.
Platform philosophy: AviadoBio maintains tight internal control over R&D and regulatory strategy. It partners with academic neurosurgery centers for procedure validation and with CROs for manufacturing and logistics. But all strategic decisions—site selection, patient recruitment, trial design—remain under Deschamps's control.
Vision: Within 5 years, AviadoBio aims to establish sub-pial thalamic injection as an accepted neurosurgical procedure for gene therapy. The procedure should become as routine as DBS (deep brain stimulation) implantation.
## 7. PAIN POINTS
Despite AVB-101's potential, Deschamps confronts significant challenges:
- Neurosurgical complexity: Not all sites are equipped to perform sub-pial injections; training and credentialing is time-consuming and expensive
- Patient recruitment challenges: FTD patients are cognitively impaired; obtaining informed consent and ensuring compliance is difficult
- Preclinical-to-clinical translation uncertainty: Animal data don't always predict human outcomes; efficacy signals in mice may not translate to patients
- Regulatory ambiguity: The FDA hasn't previously approved a gene therapy via neurosurgical delivery; regulatory path is novel and uncertain
- Manufacturing complexity: Scaling gene therapy production while maintaining GMP quality is expensive and technically challenging
- Competitive pressure: Other FTD programs (Eli Lilly tau, other gene therapies) are advancing; AviadoBio must demonstrate clinical advantage
- Patient advocacy balance: FTD advocacy groups want rapid access to AVB-101, but Deschamps must balance urgency with rigorous development
"Neurosurgery + FTD + gene therapy = a complex puzzle. Each component is hard; combining them is exponentially harder."
