Dr. Christopher Kroeger, MD, MBA
CEO & Founder, MapLight Therapeutics
## 1. Role & Scope
As CEO and Founder of MapLight Therapeutics, Dr. Kroeger leads a company built on a revolutionary platform: circuit-based neuropsychiatry drug discovery using optogenetics, spatial transcriptomics (STARmap), and systems-level neuroscience. Co-founded with Karl Deisseroth (Stanford neuroscientist and 2024 Nobel-winning optogenetics pioneer), MapLight is advancing the field of circuit mapping into therapeutic application. Dr. Kroeger oversees all strategic, scientific, clinical, and business functions, reporting to the Board and managing relationships with investors, academic collaborators, regulatory agencies, and strategic partners. His dual MD/MBA background bridges clinical medicine, neuroscience rigor, and entrepreneurial execution. His mandate is to validate circuit-based drug discovery as a paradigm and advance ML-007 (ML-007C-MA), a muscarinic agonist targeting M1/M4 circuits, through Phase 2 efficacy trials.
Key Responsibilities:
- Circuit-based drug discovery platform development and validation
- ML-007 (ML-007C-MA) clinical development strategy for schizophrenia and AD psychosis
- Strategic partnerships with academic institutions and pharma collaborators
- Regulatory pathway navigation and Phase 1/2 trial execution
## 2. Strategic Priorities
Dr. Kroeger's overarching strategic vision is to establish circuit-based neuropsychiatry as the gold standard for CNS drug discovery—a paradigm shift from target-centric (dopamine, serotonin) approaches to circuit-centric mechanistic precision. The successful Phase 1 safety and tolerability profile for ML-007 is foundational; Phase 1b/2 studies must now demonstrate efficacy in schizophrenia and neuropsychiatric symptoms of Alzheimer's disease, grounded in circuit biology. He sees the dual indication strategy as uniquely differentiated—both conditions involve prefrontal cortical dysfunction amenable to muscarinic modulation, yet each presents distinct circuit signatures. The optogenetics + STARmap + transcriptomics platform is proprietary and defensible; demonstrating its value in ML-007 will position MapLight for platform licensing and partnership opportunities. Long-term, he envisions MapLight as the circuit biology-enabled therapeutics company, with a pipeline spanning neuropsychiatric indications and potentially neurodegenerative diseases.
Immediate Objectives:
- Design and initiate Phase 1b/2 schizophrenia and AD psychosis trials with circuit biomarkers
- Advance circuit mapping platform and validate in-human circuit signatures
- Establish MapLight as thought leader in circuit-based neuropsychiatry
- Strengthen academic partnerships and platform licensing opportunities

## 4. Key Challenges & Pain Points
MapLight faces exceptional scientific and operational challenges. Circuit-based drug discovery is novel; translating optogenetics insights from rodents to human neurobiology is complex and unproven. Demonstrating clinical utility of circuit biomarkers in human patients requires sophisticated neuroimaging and electrophysiology infrastructure—expensive and operationally challenging. Phase 1 safety is promising, but efficacy data is lacking; schizophrenia has notoriously slow, variable response profiles, making trial design and endpoint selection critical and difficult. The dual indication strategy (schizophrenia + AD psychosis) adds complexity. Competitive threats from larger pharma with established schizophrenia platforms and deeper pockets are formidable. Regulatory expectations for circuit-based biomarkers are unclear; defining acceptable biomarker qualification pathways requires FDA engagement. Capital requirements are substantial; maintaining runway while advancing complex clinical programs is challenging. Investor skepticism toward early-stage circuit-based approaches may limit financing options.
Critical Pressure Points:
- Translating optogenetics insights from preclinical models to human efficacy
- Operationalizing circuit biomarkers in clinical trial settings
- Schizophrenia trial design with variable response profiles and slow onset
- Capital efficiency and runway management in complex preclinical programs
