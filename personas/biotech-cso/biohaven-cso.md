Dr. Vlad Coric, MD
CEO & Chairman, Biohaven Pharmaceuticals (Post-Pfizer)
## 1. Role & Scope
Dr. Coric is the founder and CEO of a reimagined Biohaven, emerging from its acquisition by Pfizer and the subsequent spin-off or licensing restructuring that has created an independent biotech focused on precision neurodegeneration. As a physician-scientist-entrepreneur, he uniquely bridges clinical medicine, neuroscience research, and business strategy. He oversees all strategic, scientific, and commercial functions, reporting to the Board of Directors and engaging directly with investors, regulatory authorities, and pharma partners. His role encompasses portfolio prioritization, platform development, regulatory engagement, and the organizational transformation required to rebuild Biohaven as a standalone neurodegeneration-focused entity.
Key Responsibilities:
- Strategic repositioning post-Pfizer acquisition and spin-off structuring
- Portfolio development across Kv7 (opakalin) and protein degradation platforms
- Regulatory engagement for VYGLXIA (troriluzole) SCA and ongoing Phase 3 programs
- Partnerships and licensing arrangements with Pfizer and other strategic collaborators
## 2. Strategic Priorities
Dr. Coric is intensely focused on executing Phase 3 trials for opakalin (BHV-7000) in focal epilepsy and advancing protein degradation programs for neurodegenerative indications. He views VYGLXIA as a near-term commercial asset, particularly in spinocerebellar ataxia where patient communities are tight-knit and outcomes measurable. The dual-platform strategy—leveraging both Kv7 ion channel biology and targeted protein degradation—reflects his belief in mechanistic precision. Post-Pfizer, he is prioritizing organizational independence, capital efficiency, and the narrative of a precision neuromodulation company rather than a traditional biotech. He is acutely aware of the need to differentiate from larger competitors and position Biohaven as a best-in-class team.
Immediate Objectives:
- Complete Phase 3 enrollment for opakalin focal epilepsy program
- Advance VYGLXIA launch preparation and SCA patient access programs
- Secure protein degradation partnership or advance lead candidate to IND
- Establish Biohaven as independent entity post-Pfizer with clear strategic identity
## 3. Scientific Expertise & Background
Dr. Coric earned his MD from Yale School of Medicine and completed residency and fellowship training in psychiatry with a strong neuroscience foundation. He has dual expertise: clinical psychiatry and neuropsychopharmacology on one hand, and deep knowledge of neurodegeneration mechanisms on the other. He co-founded Biohaven with the explicit mission to develop precision treatments for psychiatric and neurological disorders. His background includes leadership at major academic centers and earlier-stage biotech roles. He has published on treatment-resistant depression, OCD, and emerging neurobiological mechanisms. His entrepreneurial mindset, combined with scientific credibility, has attracted top talent and investor confidence. He has benefited from mentorship by leaders like Nick Kozauer (ex-FDA CMO), who provides strategic guidance.
Core Competencies:
- Clinical psychiatry and neuropsychopharmacology expertise
- Neurodegeneration biology and mechanistic precision targeting
- Biotech entrepreneurship and company building from founding through scale
- Regulatory pathways in CNS and rare neurological disease
## 4. Key Challenges & Pain Points
The post-Pfizer landscape presents both opportunities and profound challenges. Biohaven must now establish independence, including securing capital, building infrastructure, and asserting a unique identity in a crowded neurodegeneration space. Competition from established companies (Roche, Eli Lilly, Biogen) with massive R&D budgets is formidable, particularly in protein degradation where multiple players are active. Opakalin faces the same patient recruitment headwinds as other epilepsy programs, plus the challenge of differentiation from Xenon's azetukalner and other Kv7 entrants. VYGLXIA, while an approved commercial asset, operates in small patient populations (SCA) with limited market size. Investor sentiment toward standalone biotech is cautious; proving capital efficiency and demonstrating scientific differentiators is critical.
Critical Pressure Points:
- Establishing organizational independence and capital structure post-Pfizer
- Opakalin differentiation in competitive Kv7/epilepsy landscape
- Protein degradation program advancement without pharma-scale resources
- VYGLXIA commercialization in small, specialized patient populations

## 6. Market Understanding & Industry Perspective
Dr. Coric views the neurodegeneration space as bifurcated: major pharma chasing multibillion-dollar Alzheimer's and Parkinson's programs, while orphan neurodegenerative diseases remain underserved. He sees Biohaven's opportunity in precision targeting—both through opakalin's Kv7 mechanism and protein degradation—as differentiating from one-size-fits-all approaches. He recognizes that post-Pfizer, standalone biotech must demonstrate agility, capital efficiency, and cutting-edge science to compete. He anticipates further consolidation in neurodegeneration biotech, but believes differentiated mechanisms and strong team will attract partnership or acquisition premium. The market is increasingly receptive to biomarker-driven development and rare disease expertise.
Market Observations:
- Orphan neurodegeneration diseases are underfunded relative to major neurodegenerative pathways
- Precision medicine and biomarker strategies are increasingly valued by regulators and payers
- Standalone biotech with differentiated mechanisms command premium valuations
- Pfizer spin-off structure creates unique positioning for partnerships and M&A
