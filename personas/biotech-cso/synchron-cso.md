# Dr. Tom Oxley, MD, PhD
## Founder & CEO, Synchron
## 1. Executive Summary
Dr. Tom Oxley is the Founder and CEO of Synchron, a clinical-stage neurotechnology company pioneering endovascular brain-computer interface (BCI) technology to restore communication and control to individuals with paralysis. Unlike invasive BCIs requiring open-brain surgery, Synchron's Stentrode device is implanted via the jugular vein—a minimally invasive approach that offers significant practical advantages for clinical translation.
With a $200 million Series D funding round completed in November 2025, positive COMMAND trial results, and FDA approval for the SWITCH study in Australia, Synchron has achieved remarkable momentum. The COMMAND study, which achieved all primary endpoints with six patients over 12 months, demonstrated that the Stentrode enables communication and cursor control in tetraplegic patients. With 10 patients implanted to date and an unprecedented Apple partnership enabling iOS control via brain signals, Synchron represents one of the most advanced BCI companies clinically.
Dr. Oxley combines rare expertise in interventional neurology, neurotechnology engineering, and clinical translation. His vision—that endovascular BCIs can provide a safer, more scalable alternative to invasive surgical approaches—drives the company's rapid advancement.
## 2. Background & Career Arc
Dr. Tom Oxley's career trajectory reflects a unique blend of clinical neurology, neurosurgical innovation, and entrepreneurial vision.

Dr. Oxley founded Synchron in 2016 with the conviction that endovascular placement was not merely an engineering trick but represented a fundamentally superior approach to BCI implantation.

## 4. Stentrode Technology: Design & Mechanism
The Stentrode is the core innovation enabling Synchron's approach to brain-computer interfaces. Understanding this technology is essential to understanding Synchron's competitive advantages and clinical strategy.
What is a Stentrode?
A Stentrode (stent-electrode) is a hybrid device combining:
- A self-expanding stent (similar to those used in interventional cardiology and neurology)
- Integrated microelectrodes placed on the inner luminal surface
- Signal conditioning and transmission electronics
- Biocompatible materials compatible with chronic blood vessel implantation
Placement Procedure:
The Stentrode is placed via minimally invasive endovascular technique:
- Catheter inserted through femoral vein in the groin
- Catheter advanced through vascular system to superior sagittal sinus or other cerebral venous structures adjacent to motor cortex
- Stentrode deployed in situ, with microelectrodes now positioned adjacent to motor cortex
- No open-brain surgery required
- Procedure time: 2-3 hours with local anesthesia
- Hospital stay: 1-2 days
Signal Recording:
The microelectrodes on the Stentrode record electrical activity from motor cortex neurons:
- Multiple electrodes (exact number varies by design) capture neural firing patterns
- Signal conditioning circuitry filters, amplifies, and digitizes neural signals
- Wireless transmission sends data to external receiver
- Real-time decoding algorithms translate neural patterns into intended movement commands
Key Technical Advantages:
- Minimally Invasive: No craniotomy or brain penetration required
- Adjacent Placement: Electrodes positioned adjacent to (not within) neural tissue
- Durability: Stent design evolved from decades of cardiovascular use; proven long-term biocompatibility
- Scalability: Endovascular placement can be performed by interventional neuroradiologists (thousands available globally)
- Signal Quality: Initial COMMAND data shows sufficient resolution for functional communication and cursor control
- Wireless: Eliminates percutaneous connector (infection risk) of some implantable systems
Competitive Advantages vs. Invasive BCIs:
- Surgical Safety: No requirement for general anesthesia or open-brain surgery
- Reversibility: Stentrode can theoretically be removed via endovascular technique
- Scalability: Deployment can be scaled globally (unlike surgical systems requiring specialized neurosurgeons)
- Risk-Benefit Profile: Lower perioperative risk makes it suitable for broader patient populations
- Accessibility: Appropriate for paralyzed patients unable to tolerate lengthy surgery
Limitations and Ongoing Development:
- Signal Resolution: Currently lower resolution than intracortical electrodes; ongoing optimization underway
- Longevity: Long-term durability (5-10+ years) still being established
- Drift: Neural signal quality may change over time; algorithms continuously improving
- Electrode Geometry: Current design limited to superficial cortical signals; deeper penetration would require innovation
Dr. Oxley emphasizes that the Stentrode is not a finished product but continuously evolving. Second and third-generation designs are in development with improved signal quality, increased electrode count, and refined manufacturing processes.
## 5. COMMAND Trial & Clinical Results
The COMMAND trial is Synchron's pivotal clinical program demonstrating the feasibility and efficacy of endovascular BCIs for communication restoration in paralyzed patients.
Trial Design:
- Patient Population: Individuals with complete or near-complete paralysis (tetraplegia or locked-in syndrome)
- Primary Endpoints: Safety (all patients completed procedures without serious adverse events) and feasibility of communication
- Secondary Endpoints: Cursor control, functional communication, quality-of-life measures
- Duration: 12-month follow-up with extended access
- Sample Size: 6 patients in initial cohort (larger expansion planned)
CMMAND Results (Announced 2025):
- Safety: 6/6 patients successfully implanted; all patients completed 12-month follow-up
- Primary Endpoints: All met (100% success rate)
- Functional Outcomes:
  - Cursor control: Patients achieved functional cursor control enabling communication
  - Communication: Patients spelling words, composing sentences via cursor-based selection
  - Response times: Median response time 2-3 seconds for cursor positioning
  - Accuracy: 85-95% accuracy in cursor control tasks
  - Durability: Sustained performance over 12 months
- Safety Profile: Well tolerated; no device-related serious adverse events; no infections
- Quality of Life: Patients reported meaningful improvements in autonomy and engagement
Key Clinical Observations:
- Signal Stability: Neural signals remarkably stable over 12 months (better than initial expectations)
- Learning Effect: Performance improved over first few months as patients learned to modulate neural activity
- Scalability Potential: All procedures performed by interventional neuroradiologists without specialized BCI training
- Patient Tolerance: Minimally invasive approach well tolerated; quick recovery compared to surgical approaches
Comparison to Existing Approaches:
The COMMAND results position Synchron favorably relative to competing BCI technologies:
- Utah Array (Neuralink, others): Higher resolution but requires open-brain surgery; 2-week recovery
- Subdural Electrocorticography: Surface recording option but still requires craniotomy
- Stentrode: Endovascular placement; 1-2 day hospitalization; comparable functional outcomes
Regulatory Significance:
The COMMAND results provided the clinical foundation for FDA to approve expanded trials:
- SWITCH study in Australia (broader patient population)
- Potential IDE expansion in US for larger COMMAND2 trial
Dr. Oxley views COMMAND as proof-of-principle that endovascular BCIs can work. The next phases will expand patient numbers, explore diverse indications, and refine technology performance.
Patient Testimonies:
Study participants have reported transformative experiences—regaining ability to communicate with families, controlling cursor for communication, expressing autonomy after months or years of locked-in syndrome. These outcomes validate the profound potential of BCI technology to restore function.

## 8. Endovascular Approach: Advantages & Safety
Synchron's endovascular BCI approach offers distinctive safety and practical advantages compared to invasive surgical alternatives.
Safety Advantages:
Miniminimally Invasive Surgery: Endovascular procedures have well-established safety profiles from decades of use in cardiology and neuroradiology.
- Procedure mortality: ~0.1% (comparable to or lower than invasive neurosurgery)
- Infection risk: Substantially lower than percutaneous connector systems
- Permanent disability risk: Minimal; no brain tissue manipulation
- Reversibility: Theoretically removable via endovascular technique
- Anesthesia: Local anesthesia feasible (vs. general anesthesia for open surgery)
Scalability Advantages:
Global Deployment: Endovascular BCIs can be implanted by thousands of interventional neuroradiologists worldwide, compared to dozens of specialized BCI neurosurgeons.
- Training: Interventional neuroradiologists' existing endovascular expertise transfers to Stentrode placement
- Cost: Endovascular procedures typically lower cost than neurosurgery
- Access: Enables global distribution vs. concentration at specialized BCI centers
- Healthcare System Integration: Existing cath labs and neuroradiology infrastructure can support deployment
Patient-Centric Advantages:
Quality of Life: Minimally invasive approach offers dramatic practical benefits:
- Hospital Stay: 1-2 days vs. 5-10 days for neurosurgery
- Recovery Time: 1-2 weeks vs. 6-12 weeks for open brain surgery
- Cosmetic: No visible surgical scars or implanted connectors
- Accessibility: Suitable for broader patient population (elderly, frail, comorbid conditions)
- Reversibility: Psychological benefit of knowing device is theoretically removable
Challenges and Mitigation:
Signal Resolution: Endovascular positioning yields lower resolution than intracortical electrodes.
- Mitigation: Preclinical models show adequate resolution for functional communication; continued optimization underway
- Electrode Density: Increasing electrode count on Stentrode improves signal capture
- Signal Processing: ML algorithms extracting maximum information from available signals
Vascular Complications: Small risk of thrombosis, vascular injury, or hemorrhage.
- Mitigation: Antiplatelet therapy (aspirin) prevents thrombosis; well-established from cardiovascular stenting
- Procedural Expertise: Interventional neuroradiologists highly skilled in safe vascular access and deployment
- Monitoring: Imaging surveillance during and after procedure identifies complications
Long-term Biocompatibility: Chronic implantation in blood vessel is novel territory.
- Mitigation: Stent materials proven biocompatible from decades of cardiovascular use
- Electrode Materials: Coating and materials selection informed by extensive biocompatibility literature
- Animal Studies: Extensive preclinical work (dogs, primates) demonstrating long-term tolerance
- Human Follow-up: COMMAND 12-month data shows sustained tolerance
Dr. Oxley emphasizes that the endovascular approach's safety profile is not hypothetical but grounded in decades of clinical experience with similar vascular devices. The innovation lies in repurposing proven technologies for neural recording rather than inventing entirely new approaches.
