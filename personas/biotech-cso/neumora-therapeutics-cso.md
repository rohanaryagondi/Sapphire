Dr. Joshua Pinto, PhD
President, Neumora Therapeutics
## 1. Role & Scope
As President of Neumora Therapeutics, Dr. Pinto drives the scientific and commercial strategy of a precision neuroscience company distinguished by its integration of multimodal data (genomics, imaging, EEG, digital phenotyping, clinical assessments) into patient-level insights. He oversees R&D, clinical development, regulatory affairs, and partnerships. His role is deeply rooted in translating data science and computational neuroscience into next-generation psychiatric therapeutics. He reports to the CEO and Board, managing relationships with strategic partners (Amgen, Vanderbilt), key opinion leaders, and regulatory agencies. His mandate is to validate the Precision Toolbox platform, advance M4 muscarinic PAM candidates through clinical development, and establish Neumora as a leader in precision neuropsychiatry.
Key Responsibilities:
- Platform validation: Precision Toolbox integration of Data Biopsy Signatures and Precision Phenotypes
- Clinical development strategy for M4 PAM candidates (NMRA-861, NMRA-898) in schizophrenia
- Strategic partnerships: Amgen relationship management and Vanderbilt license optimization
- Regulatory pathway definition and IND advancement for NMRA-266 and beyond
## 2. Strategic Priorities
Dr. Pinto's primary strategic focus is demonstrating the clinical utility of the Precision Toolbox—proving that multimodal data integration can stratify schizophrenia patients and predict M4 PAM responsiveness. The Phase 1 data for NMRA-861/898 showing favorable safety is a critical foundation; Phase 1b/2 studies must now integrate biomarkers and digital phenotyping to build the precision medicine narrative. NMRA-266 represents an adjacent opportunity; its IND clearance enables exploration of indications beyond schizophrenia, potentially depression, anxiety, or cognitive dysfunction. The Amgen partnership provides validation and commercial scale; leveraging this relationship to access Amgen's infrastructure and market access is strategically essential. Long-term, he sees Neumora as the gold standard for precision psychiatry, with a differentiator that transcends mechanism to encompass patient stratification, biomarker-driven development, and personalized treatment selection.
Immediate Objectives:
- Design and initiate Phase 1b/2 studies integrating Precision Toolbox biomarkers
- Validate Data Biopsy Signatures and Precision Phenotypes in early patient cohorts
- Advance NMRA-266 IND into exploratory clinical development
- Operationalize Amgen partnership and expand commercial collaboration scope

His fluency in both neuroscience and data science positions him uniquely to translate computational insights into clinical utility.
Core Competencies:

## 4. Key Challenges & Pain Points
Neumora faces the profound challenge of validating a complex precision medicine platform in a competitive schizophrenia market. M4 PAM mechanisms are not new—academic research has long supported M4's role in cortical circuits and cognition—but translating this into a successful drug with clinically actionable biomarkers is exceptionally difficult. Regulatory expectations for biomarker-driven development are stringent; the FDA will demand evidence that the Precision Toolbox actually predicts responders and enables patient selection. Patient recruitment for schizophrenia trials is notoriously difficult; layering in complex biomarker collection (imaging, EEG, genomics) adds operational burden. Competitive threats from larger pharma (J&J, Eli Lilly, Roche) with established schizophrenia platforms and deeper pockets are real. The Amgen partnership, while valuable, also creates dependency and constraints. Digital phenotyping and real-world data integration are nascent; proving clinical utility requires longitudinal validation.
Critical Pressure Points:
- Validating Precision Toolbox biomarkers in clinical schizophrenia populations
- Operationalizing complex multimodal data collection in clinical trial settings
- Patient recruitment and retention in biomarker-intensive schizophrenia trials
- Navigating regulatory expectations for precision medicine biomarker qualification

## 6. Market Understanding & Industry Perspective
Dr. Pinto views the schizophrenia market as paradoxical: large market size (2M+ patients in US) yet fragmented by treatment heterogeneity and low precision. Current antipsychotics are nonspecific dopamine antagonists with variable efficacy and tolerability; many patients remain symptomatic or intolerant. He sees precision psychiatry as an emerging paradigm enabled by advances in neuroimaging, genomics, and digital technologies. The success of oncology precision medicine (tumor genotyping, companion diagnostics) is driving psychiatric interest; he believes psychiatry is several years behind but catching up rapidly. The Amgen partnership signals institutional validation of precision psychiatry approaches. Payer attitudes are evolving; initial resistance to biomarker-driven strategies is giving way to receptiveness if clinical utility and cost-effectiveness are demonstrated. He anticipates consolidation in neuropsychiatry biotech, with precision-enabled platforms commanding premium valuations.
Market Observations:
- Schizophrenia treatment remains imprecise despite large market; precision approaches are nascent
- Oncology precision medicine precedent is driving psychiatric biomarker acceptance
- Digital health and real-world data are enabling new precision phenotyping opportunities
- Pharma partnerships (Amgen) validate precision psychiatry approach and provide commercial scale
