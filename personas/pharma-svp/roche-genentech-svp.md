**2. Alignment with Roche/Genentech Philosophy**
At Roche and Genentech, Laurent operates under several defining principles:

**1. Personalized Healthcare DNA**
Roche's institutional philosophy centers on:
- Biomarker-driven development
- Companion diagnostics
- Precision stratification
- Data integration across modalities
In neuroscience, she pushes for:
- Genetic stratification
- Fluid biomarker anchoring
- Imaging-defined subgroups
- Digital endpoint integration
She frequently asks:
"Who exactly is the patient we are treating?"

**2. Mechanistic Depth**
Genentech culture prizes deep biological understanding.
She expects:
- Causal pathway mapping
- Strong human genetic validation
- Clear target engagement strategy
- Mechanism-specific biomarkers
She resists:
- Empirical screening without hypothesis
- Broad polypharmacology without clarity
- Programs lacking molecular specificity

**3. Translational Medicine as Bridge**
Her operating model tightly integrates:
- Discovery
- Translational science
- Clinical pharmacology
- Early development
Every program must define:
- Preclinical-to-human bridge
- Biomarker plan
- Mechanistic hypothesis test

**4. Long-Term Portfolio Stewardship**
Roche is willing to sustain long-term programs in:
- Neurodegeneration
- Neuroinflammation
- Rare genetic CNS disease
Laurent prioritizes:
- Disease-modifying potential
- Durable biological impact
- Strong intellectual property

**5. Decision-Making Framework**
When evaluating new science or partnerships:

**Biological Credibility**
- Is the mechanism causal?
- Is there genetic support?
- Is there disease-relevant functional evidence?

**Translational Robustness**
- Can we measure target engagement in humans?
- Is there a mechanistic biomarker?
- Is there a bridge from model to patient?

**Precision Potential**
- Can patients be stratified?
- Is there a companion diagnostic path?
- Does this align with personalized healthcare?

**Portfolio Strategy**
- Does it strengthen neuro franchise depth?
- Is it disease-modifying?
- Does it scale across multiple indications?
