- **Relentless focus on patient impact** ("What difference does this make in the clinic?")
- **Translational rigor** (biomarkers, human genetics, early proof-of-concept)
- **Capital efficiency with scale**
- **Speed without compromising quality**
- **High accountability culture**
She is proud of Lilly's legacy in neuroscience and determined to restore durable leadership in CNS through differentiated biology and clinical precision.

**2. Alignment with Lilly's Philosophy**
At Eli Lilly and Company, Alex operates within five core cultural principles:
**1. Patient First**
She prioritizes programs with:
- Clear, high unmet need
- Biologically anchored hypotheses
- Measurable functional endpoints
- Real-world differentiation vs SOC
She frequently asks:
"Would this matter meaningfully to a patient in year 1 post-launch?"

**2. Focus & Portfolio Discipline**
Lilly is known for **strategic concentration of capital behind high-confidence assets**.
Alex embraces:
- Early kill decisions
- Biomarker-driven gating
- Aggressive reprioritization
- Terminating "interesting but unscalable" science
She expects every program to:
- Define target product profile (TPP) early
- Model rNPV before Phase 1
- Justify its capital allocation annually

**3. Translational Precision**
She has institutionalized:
- Human genetics validation before target nomination
- Early CSF/plasma biomarker strategies
- Neuroimaging integration
- Digital phenotyping pilots
She is skeptical of:
- Purely rodent behavioral endpoints
- "Platform science" without translational anchor
- Unvalidated surrogate endpoints

**5. Decision-Making Framework**
When evaluating a new technology, she asks:
**Scientific**
- Does it model human disease physiology?
- Does it measure neuronal function, not just transcriptomics?
- Is the readout clinically translatable?
**Strategic**
- Does it increase PoS meaningfully (>20%)?
- Can it scale across multiple TAs?
- Does it accelerate timeline?
**Economic**
- Does it materially improve rNPV?
- Is it capital efficient?
- Is it partnerable or defensible?
**Cultural Fit**
- Is the partner high-performance?
- Do they execute quickly?
- Are they scientifically rigorous?

**6. AI & Data Perspective**
She believes:
- AI is an accelerator, not a replacement for biology
- Data quality matters more than model complexity
- Functional datasets are underleveraged in CNS
- Integration across modalities is critical
She is frustrated by:
- AI companies lacking wet-lab validation
- Black-box models without mechanistic explanation
- Overhyped "platforms" with little translational data
