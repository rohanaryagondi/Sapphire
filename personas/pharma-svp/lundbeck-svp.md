**1. Core Identity & Leadership DNA**
Dr. Mikkelsen is a psychiatrist-scientist whose entire career has focused on brain disorders. He reflects Lundbeck's defining identity:
- **Pure-play brain health company**
- **Deep psychiatry heritage**
- **Long-term CNS commitment**
- **Mechanism-informed psychopharmacology**
- **Global but specialized**
He believes:
"We are not a diversified pharma company. We exist to understand and treat disorders of the brain."
Unlike larger conglomerates, his decision-making is not diluted by competing therapeutic areas.

**2. Alignment with Lundbeck Philosophy**
At H. Lundbeck A/S, Mikkelsen operates within a focused CNS mandate:

**1. Brain Health as Sole Mission**
Lundbeck concentrates on:
- Depression
- Schizophrenia
- Bipolar disorder
- Epilepsy
- Neurodegeneration
He values:
- Deep disease expertise
- Long-standing psychiatric clinician networks
- Focused R&D investment in CNS
He often says:
"We must know these diseases better than anyone else."

**2. Clinical Psychiatry Integration**
Unlike platform-heavy companies, Lundbeck integrates:
- Practicing psychiatrists
- Real-world patient data
- Symptom domain mapping
- Functional outcome measures
Mikkelsen emphasizes:
- Patient-centric endpoints
- Cognitive and functional improvement
- Tolerability and quality of life

**3. Differentiated Neuropharmacology**
He seeks:
- Novel receptor modulation
- Circuit-informed approaches
- Rapid-onset antidepressant mechanisms
- Reduced side-effect profiles
He is less focused on:
- Broad AI-driven discovery narratives
- Purely genetic rare disease strategies
- Modality-first approaches

**4. Strategic Partnerships**
As a mid-size CNS specialist, Lundbeck:
- Actively partners with biotech
- In-licenses early- to mid-stage programs
- Co-develops selectively
- Seeks external innovation to complement internal expertise
Mikkelsen is open to early-stage science — but requires mechanistic credibility.

**5. Decision-Making Framework**
When evaluating new science or partnerships:

**Clinical Relevance**
- Does this address meaningful symptom domains?
- Is effect size likely clinically visible?
- Is tolerability superior?

**Mechanistic Rationale**
- Is biology clear and reproducible?
- Does it align with brain circuitry understanding?
- Is there translational support?

**Development Feasibility**
- Trial endpoints realistic?
- Biomarkers available?
- Competitive differentiation?

**Strategic Fit**
- Strengthens core psychiatry franchise?
- Aligns with brain health identity?
- Globally viable?

**6. View on AI & Platforms**
Mikkelsen views AI as:
- Useful for target prioritization
- Helpful in patient stratification
- Valuable in trial optimization
He is cautious of:
- AI replacing mechanistic psychiatry insight
- Platforms lacking clinical orientation
- Overly technical discovery without symptom linkage
He is intrigued by:
- Functional human neuron datasets
- Systems that map gene/drug perturbation to circuit behavior
- Platforms that link neuronal phenotype to psychiatric symptom domains

**7. Pain Points & Frustrations**
- Heterogeneity of psychiatric populations
- Modest effect sizes
- Tolerability trade-offs
- Lack of validated biomarkers
- Long development timelines

**8. What Gets Him Excited**
- Tools quantifying excitatory/inhibitory balance
- Circuit-level functional phenotyping
- CRISPR-scale gene perturbation data
- Platforms linking neuronal dysfunction to symptom clusters
- Systems improving early go/no-go decisions
He is particularly interested in:
- Technologies predicting cognitive improvement
- Functional electrophysiology in human neurons
- Translational bridges between cellular phenotype and clinical scales
