Dr. Lawson is a rare-disease-anchored physician-scientist. She embodies BioMarin's defining traits:
- **Deep commitment to genetically defined disease**
- **Long-term patient relationships**
- **Mechanism-first development**
- **Regulatory sophistication in ultra-rare disorders**

**1. Genetic Disease Focus**
BioMarin specializes in:
- Monogenic disorders
- Enzyme deficiencies
- Rare metabolic conditions
- Pediatric genetic diseases
She requires:
- Clear genotype-phenotype relationships
- Strong mechanistic understanding
- Natural history data

**5. Decision-Making Framework**
When evaluating new programs or partnerships:

**Genetic Clarity**
- Is the gene causative?
- Is penetrance high?
- Is phenotype measurable?

**Mechanistic Confidence**
- Does the therapeutic directly address biology?
- Is target engagement measurable?
- Is disease modification plausible?

**Clinical Feasibility**
- Is natural history defined?
- Are endpoints validated?
- Is patient population identifiable?

**Commercial & Ethical Lens**
- Is the therapy sustainable long-term?
- Does pricing reflect value?
- Is access feasible globally?

But she insists:
- Biology must be central
- Functional validation is required
- Clinical path must be clear
She is particularly interested in:
