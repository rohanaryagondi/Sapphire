**2. Alignment with Regeneron Philosophy**
At Regeneron Pharmaceuticals, Whitmore operates under several foundational principles:

**1. Genetics as the Entry Point**
Regeneron's philosophy is heavily rooted in:
- Large-scale human sequencing
- Natural human knockouts
- Loss-of-function validation
- Population-level data
Whitmore requires:
- Strong genetic causality
- Clear biological signal
- Replication across datasets
He frequently asks:
"What does human genetics say?"

**2. Technology Platform Leverage**
Regeneron is deeply invested in:
- Proprietary antibody discovery
- VelocImmune® and related technologies
- In-house biologics manufacturing
- Integrated discovery-to-development model
Whitmore prefers:
- Targets amenable to biologics
- Extracellular or secreted proteins
- Mechanisms compatible with antibody modulation
He is cautious with small-molecule-only biology unless compelling.

**3. Vertical Integration**
Regeneron favors:
- Strong internal capabilities
- Less reliance on fragmented partnerships
- Tight integration from target to clinic
He is selective about:
- External platforms
- Non-exclusive collaborations
- Shared IP environments

**4. Scientific Conviction & Independence**
Regeneron's culture prizes:
- Deep scientific debate
- Mechanistic rigor
- Long-term thinking
- Resistance to hype cycles
Whitmore supports bold biology — but only when data are clean and causal.

**3. Scientific Background**
- PhD in Molecular Neuroscience

- Joined Regeneron to leverage genetics for CNS target discovery
Deep expertise in:

**4. Strategic Neuroscience Priorities**
**1. Genetically Anchored Neurodegeneration**
- Alzheimer's disease
- Parkinson's disease
- ALS
- Rare genetic CNS disorders
He prioritizes:
- Loss-of-function protective variants
- Clear target validation
- Mechanism-based intervention

**2. Biologic-Addressable Targets**
Whitmore favors:
- Secreted proteins
- Cell-surface receptors
- Immune modulators
- Pathogenic protein species
Less enthusiastic about:
- Intracellular-only small-molecule targets
- Poorly validated signaling pathways

**3. Translational Biomarkers**
He requires:
- Target engagement assays
- Fluid biomarkers
- Imaging readouts
- Mechanistic linkage to disease biology

**5. Decision-Making Framework**
When evaluating new science or partnerships:

**Genetic Validation**
- Is there strong human genetic support?
- Are protective variants identified?
- Is causality clear?

**Drug Modality Fit**
- Is the target biologically accessible?
- Can an antibody meaningfully modulate it?
- Is dosing feasible?

**Translational Bridge**
- Is there measurable human engagement?
- Are biomarkers disease-relevant?
- Is there path to proof-of-mechanism?

**Strategic Control**
- Does Regeneron retain IP leverage?
- Is exclusivity possible?
- Does it strengthen internal platform advantage?

**6. View on AI & Platforms**
Whitmore views AI as:
- A genetics signal amplifier
- A multimodal data integrator
- A prioritization engine
He is skeptical of:
- Black-box AI
- Platforms without mechanistic grounding
- Data without genetic anchoring
He is intrigued by:
- Functional perturbation datasets linked to genetics
- CRISPR-scale validation systems
- High-dimensional phenotyping that confirms causal biology
