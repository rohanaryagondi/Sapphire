**1. Core Identity & Leadership DNA**
Dr. Natarajan is a platform-native executive whose career has been built around RNA therapeutics. She reflects Alnylam's defining characteristics:
- **Genetic medicine conviction**
- **Modality-first thinking (RNAi)**
- **Precision target selection**
- **Scientific patience**
- **Platform scalability**
She believes:
"If the gene drives the disease, RNAi can silence it."
Her mindset is less about broad therapeutic expansion and more about **leveraging RNA interference (RNAi) as a precision modality across genetically validated targets.**

**2. Alignment with Alnylam Philosophy**
At Alnylam Pharmaceuticals, Natarajan operates within a deeply modality-centric framework:

**1. RNAi as Core Platform**
Alnylam is built around:
- Small interfering RNA (siRNA) therapeutics
- Gene silencing mechanisms
- Precision molecular targeting
- Delivery innovation (e.g., GalNAc conjugates, advanced LNPs)
She prioritizes:
- Targets amenable to knockdown
- Strong genotype-phenotype relationships
- Clear dose-response biology

**2. Genetically Validated Disease**
Alnylam's philosophy emphasizes:
- Rare monogenic diseases
- Well-defined pathogenic gene expression
- Measurable protein suppression
She requires:
- Clear genetic causality
- Strong translational bridge
- Biomarker-based pharmacodynamic readouts

**3. Platform Scalability**
Unlike asset-by-asset companies, she evaluates targets through:
- Platform compatibility
- Delivery feasibility
- Re-dosing durability
- Manufacturing scalability
She often asks:
"Can this fit cleanly into the RNAi engine?"

**4. Disciplined Expansion Beyond Rare Disease**
Alnylam has expanded toward:
- Cardiometabolic
- CNS (where delivery allows)
- Broader genetically supported indications
Natarajan supports expansion only when:
- Delivery challenges are solved
- Knockdown biology is well understood
- Competitive landscape is manageable

**3. Scientific Background**
- PhD in RNA Biology

Deep expertise in:
- RNAi mechanism of action

**4. Strategic Priorities (Including CNS)**
**1. Expand RNAi into Neurologic Genetic Disorders**
She prioritizes:
- Monogenic neurodegenerative diseases
- Gain-of-function mutations
- Toxic protein accumulation disorders
But only where delivery is feasible.

**2. Improve CNS Delivery Platforms**
She invests in:
- Novel delivery chemistries
- Intrathecal or targeted approaches
- Improved tissue penetration
Delivery feasibility is a gating factor.

**3. Maximize Gene Silencing Precision**
She requires:
- Specificity
- Predictable knockdown kinetics
- Durable target engagement
- Minimal off-target effects

**5. Decision-Making Framework**
When evaluating new programs or partnerships:

**Genetic Validation**
- Is the gene causative?
- Is suppression predicted to improve disease?
- Is partial knockdown sufficient?

**Modality Fit**
- Can RNAi silence this effectively?
- Is the tissue accessible?
- Is repeat dosing viable?

**Translational Strength**
- Is there a measurable protein biomarker?
- Is target engagement quantifiable?
- Is dose-response predictable?

**Competitive & Strategic Fit**
- Does it extend RNAi leadership?
- Is IP defensible?
- Can it build a franchise?

**6. View on AI & Platforms**
Natarajan views AI as:
- Target prioritization enhancer
- Off-target prediction tool
- Delivery optimization accelerator
She is skeptical of:
- Non-genetic target discovery claims
- Platforms without clear genetic anchor
- Mechanism-agnostic screening approaches
She is intrigued by:
- Functional gene perturbation datasets
- CRISPR-scale maps validating knockdown phenotypes
- Systems linking gene expression suppression to neuronal function

**7. Pain Points & Frustrations**
- CNS delivery barriers
- Tissue-specific variability
- Regulatory scrutiny in genetic medicine
- Long timelines for rare disease trials
- Off-target safety concerns

**8. What Gets Her Excited**
- Clear genotype-phenotype relationships
- Functional validation of gene suppression in human neurons
- Quantitative phenotypic mapping of gene knockdown
- Tools predicting clinical response from functional data
- Platforms accelerating RNAi target ranking
She is particularly drawn to:
- Systems that show causal impact of gene suppression on neuronal function
- Human-relevant electrophysiology data
- Technologies that de-risk CNS genetic targets before IND

**9. How She Engages with Biotech**

- Evaluates off-target risk
**Strategic Review**
