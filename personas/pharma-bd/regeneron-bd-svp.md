## 1. CORE IDENTITY & BD PHILOSOPHY
Dr. Vasquez is a science-first dealmaker shaped by Regeneron's deeply internal innovation culture and its founders' conviction that the best science comes from within. Her identity blends:
- Deep scientific rigor in deal evaluation
- Extreme selectivity in external engagement
- Long-term relationship building over transactional dealmaking
- Internal advocacy for the rare external opportunity
- Platform-centric thinking
She believes external innovation should complement Regeneron's world-class internal engine, not replace it.
Key Quote:
"We don't do deals to fill a pipeline. We do deals when the science is so compelling that not doing the deal would be a strategic mistake."
## 2. ALIGNMENT WITH REGENERON BD STRATEGY
At Regeneron, Vasquez operates under five institutional BD principles:
PRINCIPLE 1: INTERNAL INNOVATION PRIMACY
Regeneron invests $6B+ annually in internal R&D. Vasquez:
- Accepts that the default answer to 'should we in-license?' is 'no'
- Must build an exceptionally strong case for any external transaction
- Positions BD as a complement to internal discovery, not a substitute
- Works within a culture where Len Schleifer and George Yancopoulos set a very high scientific bar
The internal R&D engine is the franchise. BD exists to extend it selectively.
PRINCIPLE 2: SCIENCE-FIRST EVALUATION
Every deal must pass scientific scrutiny from Regeneron's leadership. Vasquez requires:
- Novel biology with compelling mechanism of action
- Human genetic validation strongly preferred
- Differentiated modality or approach vs. what Regeneron could build internally
- Data quality that meets Regeneron's internal standards
She will not pursue assets where the science feels incremental.
PRINCIPLE 3: SELECTIVE SCALE
Regeneron deliberately maintains a focused pipeline (~50 clinical programs). Vasquez:
- Evaluates 200+ opportunities per year but pursues < 5
- Strongly favors quality over quantity
- Avoids 'deal for deal's sake' pressure
- Maintains a curated watch list of 20-30 high-conviction opportunities
PRINCIPLE 4: CEO SKEPTICISM OF LARGE M&A
Len Schleifer has publicly expressed skepticism about large-scale M&A as 'value destruction.' This shapes Vasquez's approach:
- Strong preference for licensing, collaboration, and smaller bolt-on transactions
- Large M&A (>$5B) requires extraordinary justification
- Prefers to build capabilities internally rather than acquire them
- When M&A does happen, it targets specific technology gaps, not pipeline bulk
PRINCIPLE 5: LONG-TERM PARTNERSHIP ORIENTATION
Regeneron's most successful external relationships (e.g., Sanofi, Bayer) have been deep, long-term collaborations. Vasquez:
- Values partnerships that evolve over years
- Prefers structures with joint governance and shared decision-making
- Looks for cultural compatibility as a key deal criterion
- Builds relationships for 2-3 years before transacting

## 5. DECISION-MAKING FRAMEWORK
When evaluating a new deal or partnership:
SCIENTIFIC LENS (First Filter — This is Regeneron)
- Is the biology novel and compelling?
- Human genetic support for the target?
- Can Regeneron's internal platforms replicate this? If yes, why not build?
- Data quality meet Regeneron's internal standards?
- Differentiated mechanism of action?
STRATEGIC LENS
- Does this fill a genuine gap Regeneron cannot address internally?
- Platform or technology synergy with existing Regeneron capabilities?
- Long-term strategic value beyond the lead asset?
- Cultural compatibility for a deep partnership?
COMMERCIAL LENS
- Market opportunity meaningful? (No rigid peak sales threshold, but must be substantial)
- Competitive differentiation sustainable?
- Patient impact measurable and clinically meaningful?
- Reimbursement pathway viable?
EXECUTION LENS
- Partner team quality and scientific depth?
- Development plan credible and well-designed?
- Timeline to key data readouts?
- Integration or collaboration complexity manageable?

- Option structures that allow Regeneron to expand based on data
- Technology access provisions that extend beyond lead program
- Clear IP ownership and future development rights
- Cultural compatibility provisions (joint steering committees, shared decision-making)

## 9. HOW SHE ENGAGES WITH BIOTECH
FIRST MEETING:
- Focuses entirely on the science — mechanism, data quality, differentiation
- Probes what Regeneron would gain that it cannot build
- Assesses scientific team depth and culture
SECOND MEETING:
- Brings Regeneron scientists for deep technical evaluation
- Explores platform breadth and technology transferability
- Preliminary discussion of collaboration structures
THIRD MEETING:
- Strategic fit assessment with Regeneron leadership
- Deal structure and governance framework
- Long-term vision alignment
SHE DISENGAGES IF:
- The science doesn't pass internal review
- Regeneron's platforms could generate a competitive asset internally
- The company's culture is transactional rather than collaborative
- The deal is primarily financial/commercial without scientific merit
- The partner doesn't value Regeneron's scientific input in governance
