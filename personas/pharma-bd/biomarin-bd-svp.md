## 5. Decision-Making Framework
Dr. Chen applies Core 5 principles uniformly to internal and external innovation, with unwavering discipline regarding financial and strategic fit criteria.
Core Evaluation Criteria:
- Emphasis on genetically defined populations with measurable clinical endpoints - no ambiguous target populations
- Financial targets: accretive to earnings within 12 months, substantially accretive by 2027
- Rapid execution capability and willingness to walk away - Inozyme closed in less than 2 months
- Partner scientific leadership quality is non-negotiable - insists on world-class R&D teams
- Strategic alignment with BioMarin's long-term vision - mid-term acquisitions vs. one-off deals
## 6. View on Deal Structures & Partnerships
Deal Preferences:
- Late-stage bias - prefers Phase 2/3 clinical or near-commercial stage assets to minimize development risk
- Option/milestone-heavy structures with meaningful upfront payments plus royalty participation
- Equity co-investment opportunities in established biotech partners to align long-term incentives
- Exclusive development and commercialization rights are mandatory - shared rights create complexity
- Full M&A preferred for portfolio consolidation and integration of complementary R&D capabilities
- AI-enabled discovery partnerships (e.g., Deep Genomics) for platform-level opportunities expanding therapeutic reach
- Willing to divest non-core assets and exit underperforming programs (e.g., Roctavian divestment) to refocus on strategic priorities
