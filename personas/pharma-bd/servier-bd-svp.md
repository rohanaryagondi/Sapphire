# 4. Strategic BD Priorities in Neuroscience
Neuroscience is Servier's designated growth frontier:
Neuropsychiatry (Primary): Treatment-resistant depression (agomelatine/Valdoxan legacy), schizophrenia (negative symptoms, cognitive impairment), anxiety (novel non-benzo mechanisms), autism spectrum (targeted approaches for defined subtypes), dual diagnosis (neuropsych + cardiometabolic comorbidity).
Rare Neurological Diseases: Fragile X (Kaerus partnership), Rett syndrome, Angelman syndrome, genetically defined neurodevelopmental disorders, gene therapy for monogenic CNS conditions.
Neuroscience Platforms: RNA-targeting (Base4), human neuronal functional screening, BBB delivery technologies, biomarker discovery for neuropsychiatric stratification, AI/ML for target discovery and trial optimization.
Neuroscience in Aging: Cognitive decline with cardiometabolic disease, vascular contributions to dementia, neuroprotection in metabolic dysfunction, CNS-metabolic convergence leveraging Servier's cardiometabolic legacy.
# 5. Decision-Making Framework
Patient Impact Lens (First Filter — This is Servier): Significant unmet need? Meaningfully change outcomes? Servier's long-term horizon makes a difference? Foundation governance creates advantage?
Scientific Lens: Compelling MOA with biological rationale? Human translational evidence? Differentiated approach? Data quality?
Strategic Lens: Fits therapeutic area priorities? Platform value beyond lead? Builds neuroscience franchise credibility? Cultural compatibility?
Practical Lens: Development timeline and cost? Regulatory pathway clarity (EMA + FDA)? Global feasibility? Partner team quality?
Note: Financial threshold is lower than Big Pharma — peak sales $500M+ can be attractive if patient impact is high.

# 9. How He Engages with Biotech
First Meeting:
Focuses on unmet patient need and scientific rationale. Shares Servier's story (foundation governance, long-term orientation). Assesses cultural compatibility. Explores how Servier's advantages apply.
Second Meeting:
Deeper scientific review with R&D team. Development strategy and regulatory discussion. Preliminary collaboration framework. Platform breadth and multi-program potential.
Third Meeting:
Deal terms and financial structure. Manufacturing and supply chain. Long-term vision alignment. Internal governance approval process.
Disengages if:
Pure auction where Servier can't differentiate
Science doesn't address meaningful unmet need
Company wants a check not a partner
Poor cultural fit
Indication too commercially crowded
