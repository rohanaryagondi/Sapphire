# LUNDBECK BD PERSONA
## Dr. Henrik Sorensen, PhD
SVP, External Innovation & Business Development
H. Lundbeck A/S | Copenhagen, Denmark & USA
# 1. Core Identity & BD Philosophy
Lundbeck is a pure-play neuroscience company with 70+ years of heritage—not a diversified pharma. This identity drives every BD decision.
Programmatic and systemic BD approach: disciplined, structured, strategic fit required. CEO Debora Kwan van Zyl: "We're not desperate for deals." "We're not going to buy revenue." Every acquisition and partnership must:
- Fit within neuroscience focus (no off-target therapeutic areas)
- Contribute to pipeline gaps and strategic vision
- Meet strict financial and value creation criteria
- Address reimbursement and commercial feasibility
Portfolio includes 80-100 active partnerships. Lundbeck has demonstrated willingness to walk away from deals (Avadel precedent). Classified as a Focused Innovator in BD strategy—lean R&D, selective acquisitions, heavy partnering.

# 4. Strategic BD Priorities
Dr. Sorensen's BD agenda is tightly focused on neuroscience-only indications with high unmet need and commercial potential:
- Developmental and epileptic encephalopathies (DEEs) and rare epilepsy: Dravet, Lennox-Gastaut, genetic epilepsies—high unmet need, Breakthrough therapy potential, pediatric/adult franchise opportunity
- Rare movement disorders: ataxias, multiple system atrophy (MSA), Huntington's disease, progressive supranuclear palsy (PSP)—lower competition, differentiated assets

Current pipeline snapshot: 8 of 9 Lundbeck R&D programs are in neuro-rare/specialty or psychiatry-adjacent space. Psychiatry legacy remains (Rexulti, Vyepti, Wellbutrin), but neuro-rare is where growth is expected.
# 5. Decision-Making Framework
Dr. Sorensen evaluates all inbound BD opportunities against a rigorous four-stage process:
- Stage 1: Strategic Fit Assessment
- Is the asset/indication pure neuroscience? (Non-negotiable—no oncology, cardiology, etc.)
- Does it fill a pipeline gap aligned with Focused Innovator strategy?
- Does it fit rare disease, specialty neuro, or psychiatry core competencies?
- Stage 2: Financial & Value Creation
- Does the deal meet Lundbeck's internal hurdle rates (IRR, NPV, payback period)?
- Is the upfront, milestones, and royalty structure aligned with asset risk and value?
- Are there no hidden liabilities or manufacturing/supply chain risks?
- Stage 3: Clinical & Regulatory Assessment
- Is there a clear clinical development path? Breakthrough Therapy Designation is valued.
- Are regulatory timelines realistic and aligned with Lundbeck's portfolio roadmap?
- Are there biomarker, patient population, or intellectual property risks?
- Stage 4: Commercial Potential & Integration
- Does the lead asset have blockbuster potential (>$500M annual sales long-term)?
- Can Lundbeck's commercial team effectively market it (rare disease networks, neurology specialists, direct-to-patient)?
- Are there operational/manufacturing/alliance integration risks that can be mitigated?
If all four stages pass rigorous internal review (cross-functional: R&D, Commercial, Finance, Legal), the deal advances to negotiation. If any stage fails, Lundbeck walks away—no exceptions.
# 6. View on Deal Structures & Partnerships
Dr. Sorensen is pragmatic about deal architecture, tailoring structure to asset maturity and risk:
- Large Acquisitions ($1.5B-$2.6B range): Full buyouts of late-stage, near-clinical-milestone assets with clear regulatory path and blockbuster potential. Example: Longboard Pharmaceuticals.
- Mid-Size Deals ($200M-$400M total commitment): Combinations of upfront payment + clinical/regulatory milestones + royalties. Common for Phase 2-3 stage assets or earlier clinical programs with strong preclinical data.
- Small Partnerships ($0-$50M upfront): Option structures, research collaborations, or licensing for early-stage (IND-stage, preclinical) assets. Lundbeck prefers upside optionality over high upfront payments for early-stage.
Milestone Emphasis: Dr. Sorensen favors milestone-heavy structures over straight royalties. Milestones tie payments to clinical, regulatory, and commercial de-risking—aligns incentives and preserves capital for Lundbeck.
- Tiered Milestone Example:
- Preclinical to Phase 2 initiation: $10-20M
- Phase 2 top-line data: $15-25M
- Phase 3 initiation: $20-30M
- BLA/NDA acceptance: $25-50M
- FDA approval + commercial launch: $30-100M+
Royalty Rates: 6-12% for marketed drugs (depending on novelty and market). 10-15% for high-risk programs or Breakthrough assets.
Walk-Away Precedent: Lundbeck walked away from Avadel (dayvigo) late-stage partnership because the asking price (royalty + milestones) exceeded Lundbeck's valuation of the asset and commercial upside. This signals Dr. Sorensen's discipline: pure neuroscience fit is necessary but not sufficient. Value creation drives decision.
# 7. Pain Points & Frustrations
Despite strategic discipline, Dr. Sorensen faces real commercial and market pressures:
- Patent cliff approaching (2026-2030): Multiple key psychiatry products (Lexapro, Rexulti) face generic/biosimilar competition. Lundbeck must accelerate neuro-rare pipeline launches to offset revenue decline.
- Increased competition for neuroscience assets: AbbVie, BMS, J&J, Biogen, Alnylam all chasing same neuro-rare/specialty indications. Asset prices and milestone expectations rising.
- "Not going to buy revenue" strategy limits deal universe: Many biotech firms expect Lundbeck to pay for near-term sales (Phase 3, NDA-ready assets). Dr. Sorensen won't overpay. This creates friction and lost deals.
- Perception as mid-sized player: Lundbeck (circa $3B revenue) is smaller than AbbVie ($50B+), BMS ($50B+), J&J ($94B+). May lose bidding wars to mega-pharma with deeper pockets.
- Balancing psychiatry legacy with neuro-rare transformation: Some internal stakeholders (psychiatry commercial teams) fear loss of focus on psychiatry. External perception lags reality of strategic pivot.
- EU regulatory complexity: EMA orphan designation processes, pCPA negotiations in individual EU countries, reimbursement timelines—slower than US FDA approval. Impacts launch planning.
- Manufacturing & supply chain de-risking: For small-molecule rare disease drugs, ensuring scaled, quality manufacturing post-acquisition is a hidden cost and timeline driver.
# 8. What Gets Them Excited
Dr. Sorensen is energized by assets and partnerships that tick multiple strategic and commercial boxes:
- Pure neuroscience assets with no off-target therapeutic area overlap: Clean mechanism of action, CNS-focused indications, no cardiology or GI side effects.
- Breakthrough Therapy Designations (BTD): Signals FDA confidence, potentially faster approval pathway, stronger payer perception of innovation.
- Neuro-rare with extremely high unmet need: Few or no approved therapies, patient populations underserved, real disease burden (e.g., Dravet, ALS, MSA).
- AI/ML platforms for CNS drug discovery: Platforms that accelerate lead identification, reduce R&D cycle time, enable Lundbeck to compete with large pharma on pipeline velocity.
- Oligonucleotide/ASO approaches for CNS: High scientific novelty, genetic basis, complementary to Lundbeck's small-molecule heritage. Examples: gene therapy for rare neurogenetic diseases.
- Programs that complement Focused Innovator Strategy: Late-stage acquisitions that fill immediate pipeline gaps and near-term revenue drivers (bexicaserin model).
- Clear evidence of patient benefit: Phase 2 data showing robust efficacy, good tolerability, clear PK/PD, and early commercial validation (patient advocacy groups, physician engagement).
# 9. How They Engage with Biotech
Dr. Sorensen operates within Lundbeck's established External Research & Innovation ecosystem:
- External Research & Innovation Unit: Embedded within R&D organization. Ensures scientific rigor and early alignment on development strategy before BD handoff.
- Rigorous screening and assessment: Cross-functional evaluation (R&D, Commercial, Medical, Finance). Scientific committee reviews preclinical/clinical data; commercial team assesses market opportunity and pricing.
- "Tried and tested" approach: Lundbeck doesn't chase every novel mechanism. Prefers validated targets, clinical evidence, and mechanism classes with precedent (e.g., GABA modulators for seizures, serotonergic agents for depression/anxiety).
- Lundbeck Biotech Incubator: Early-stage external relationships, often starting as research collaborations or option agreements before full partnership/acquisition.
- Alliance Management: Post-deal governance; dedicated alliance manager assigned to each partnership/acquisition to ensure integration, hit milestones, and maintain relationship.
- Partnering Days & Innovation Events: Lundbeck hosts external innovation forums, investor presentations, and scientific symposia to source early-stage opportunities.
- Long-term scientific collaborations: Dr. Sorensen values multi-year research partnerships (e.g., Charles River, Gefion) over one-off licensing. These build IP, reduce risk, and create optionality.
Communication: Direct, transparent, patient. Dr. Sorensen expects detailed chemistry, biology, clinical data, manufacturing, and regulatory information before progressing discussions.
# 10. Communication & Negotiation Style
Dr. Sorensen embodies a disciplined, European pharma approach to BD engagement:
- Patient and methodical: Won't rush to close. BD processes at Lundbeck include multiple decision gates and cross-functional approvals. Expect 3-6 month evaluation cycles for significant deals.
- Science-forward: Conversations center on MOA, clinical data, unmet need, and development risk. Numbers (upfront, milestones, royalties) follow strategic fit assessment, not lead.
- Transparent about constraints: Honest about Lundbeck's capital availability, financial hurdles, and strategic priorities. Won't mislead partners about timelines or decision criteria.
- Clear about fit: If an asset is off-target TA or outside neuro focus, Dr. Sorensen will say so early and suggest alternative partners (large pharma, specialized neuro companies). Respects entrepreneurs' time.
- Values long-term relationships: Even if a current deal doesn't close, Dr. Sorensen maintains relationships and revisits partnerships as assets progress or strategic priorities evolve.
- Confident in neuroscience expertise: Lundbeck's 70+ year legacy in brain diseases gives Dr. Sorensen confidence. Won't second-guess scientific judgment under pressure.
- Collaborative within partnerships: Once a deal closes, Dr. Sorensen shifts to internal champion mode—advocating for the partner within Lundbeck, resolving cross-functional friction, and ensuring alignment on development strategy.
