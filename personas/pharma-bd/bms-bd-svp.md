## 5. Decision-Making Framework
Paul applies rigorous, five-pillar framework to BD evaluation. Each pillar is weighted equally in initial screening; weighting adjusts by deal stage and risk profile.
- **Scientific Merit:** Is this the right science? Does BMS add genuine value? Novel mechanism, robust preclinical data, expert validation.
- **Strategic Alignment:** First-in-class or best-in-class? Fills a therapeutic gap in BMS portfolio? Synergistic with existing assets (e.g., Cobenfy combinations)?
- **Medical & Clinical Viability:** Can BMS execute? World-class trial operations, neuroimaging capabilities, patient access, regulatory pathway clarity?
- **Business & Commercial Fit:** Peak sales potential? Competitive differentiation? Market size, payer landscape, reimbursement likelihood?
- **Deal Economics & Partnership Fit:** Risk-adjusted valuation, milestone structure, IP clarity, partner's long-term vision alignment with BMS. Reciprocal value creation over 10+ years.

## 6. View on Deal Structures & Partnerships
Paul is known for creative, customized deal structures. No template. Every partnership is tailored to partner's stage, capital needs, IP position, and long-term goals.
- **Mega-Acquisitions ($10B+):** Karuna ($14B) model. Best for transformational, late-stage assets with clear regulatory path and commercial potential. Integration risk offset by immediate revenue contribution.
- **Large Licensing ($1B-$3B total value):** BioArctic ($1.35B) model. Upfront + milestone + royalty tiers. Founder/investors retain equity; BMS gets exclusive global rights. De-risks asset while maintaining founder motivation.
- **Mid-Size Deals ($80M-$617M):** Prothena model. Program-specific licensing, option structures, or minority investment. Allows BMS to 'test drive' partnership before scaling.
- **Milestone-Heavy Structures:** Regulatory & commercial milestones tied to Go/No-Go gates. Aligns incentives; partners earn upside on success.
- **Joint Governance:** Steering committees with BMS + partner representation. Transparency, co-decision making, conflict resolution. Builds trust.
- **Option Structures (Early-Stage):** BMS funds discovery, retains opt-in rights for preclinical/Phase 1 success. Low upfront risk; partner retains autonomy.
- **Geographic Flexibility:** Co-development in China/Asia; exclusive US/EU to BMS. Accommodates partner's regional interests.

## 7. Pain Points & Frustrations
Despite headline successes, Paul faces real operational and strategic pressures that shape his outlook and BD priorities.
- **Patent Cliff Urgency:** Exclusivity loss on blockbusters (Opdivo, Keytruda loss pending). Makes BD non-negotiable; but raises internal pressure to close deals quickly, sometimes at premium valuations.
- **Cobenfy Commercialization Underperformance:** $10M in Q4 2024 sales fell short of internal projections ($50M+ expected). Paul owns this challenge; slower-than-expected market adoption of muscarinic agonist in schizophrenia. Impacts confidence in Karuna upside.
- **Integration Bandwidth:** Managing Karuna, BioArctic, Prothena, Evotec, Schrödinger simultaneously. Integration teams stretched; post-acquisition execution risk high.
- **Maintaining Deal Quality at Volume:** Closing 75+ partnerships annually while ensuring scientific fit, strategic alignment, and commercial viability is exhausting. Risk of 'deal for deal's sake.'
- **Neuroscience Portfolio Execution Post-Karuna:** Can BMS deliver on expansions, combinations, and new indications? Execution risk on Cobengy psychosis expansion, anti-amyloid/tau combo potential, biomarker integration.
- **Cost-Cutting vs. BD Investment Tension:** CFO pressure to improve margins conflicts with Paul's need to fund deals, maintain partnerships, and invest in integration. Constraints impact deal execution speed and partner confidence.

- **% Pipeline Externally Sourced:** Target >60%. Reflects BD effectiveness in filling portfolio; correlates with growth and innovation.
- **Partnership Count & Landscape:** Target 75+ active partnerships. Indicates ecosystem breadth and influence.
- **Deal Volume & Value:** Tracks # of deals closed/year, total value, average deal size, mix (licensing vs. acquisition vs. collaboration).
- **Cobenfy Revenue Trajectory:** Quarterly revenue, Rx growth rate, market share vs. Abilify. Critical metric post-$14B Karuna investment.
- **Phase 3 Success Rate:** Tracks % of BD-sourced Phase 3 programs that achieve primary endpoints. Measures due diligence quality.

- **Portfolio Balance (Internal vs. External):** Early/mid-stage internal programs vs. late-stage licensed/acquired assets. Ensures pipeline sustainability.
- **Growth Portfolio Contribution (GPC):** % of company revenue from products launched in past 5 years. Measures BD's impact on top-line growth.
