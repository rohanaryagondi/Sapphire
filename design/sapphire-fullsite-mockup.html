◆ Synthesis — the recommendation

Three genes are credibly supported for TSC2-rescue investigation, ordered by confidence: DCTN6 (strongest EP-reversal, partial mechanistic grounding via dynactin/axonal-transport/mTOR axis), FZD7 (WNT/mTOR crosstalk documented in patient-derived neurons — KOL's preferred candidate clinically), and RALA (small GTPase with mTOR-complex assembly role, EP-reversal 3rd). Confidence: Low → Medium (EP-reversal is real; causal mechanisms are hypothesis-level; roundtable split 2A / 2C)
⊕ Proposed experiment

Mechanistic validation screen: in TSC2-null neurons (patient iPSC-derived), transiently overexpress DCTN6, FZD7, and RALA individually. Readouts: (1) mTORC1 activity by pS6K T389; (2) cell size normalization; (3) mTOR pathway phosphoproteomics. Positive control: rapamycin 10 nM. This converts the EP-reversal correlation into causal evidence or eliminates the candidates — resolving the KNOWN_UNKNOWN for the top 3 and directly answering the adversarial partner's objection.
