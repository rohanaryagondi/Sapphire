PIPELINE_140.docx
=================
PROMPT: "Identify MoAs crowded by generics."

STAGE SUMMARY:
Stage 1: Data Ingestion & Regulatory Mapping
  Purpose: Aggregate comprehensive CNS drug approval records from FDA, EMA, and 
  global regulatory databases.
  Key Inputs: FDA Orange Book, EMA EPAR, patent registries, ClinicalTrials.gov
  Key Tools: FDA Orange Book API, EMA Database, USPTO Search, Espacenet
  Sample Output: Structured table of CNS drugs with approval date, patent 
  expiration, generic entry date.

Stage 2: MoA Classification & Pharmacology Standardization
  Purpose: Standardize mechanism of action classifications using controlled 
  vocabularies and expert pharmacology rules.
  Key Inputs: Drug database, FDA labels, PubMed literature, receptor data
  Key Tools: PubMed NLP Parser, DrugBank API, ChEMBL, FDA Label Parser
  Sample Output: MoA classification table with confidence scores, receptor 
  binding profiles, literature support.

Stage 3: Generic Penetration & Market Share Analysis
  Purpose: Quantify generic drug availability, market share concentration, and 
  brand-to-generic conversion timelines for each MoA.
  Key Inputs: Drug inventory, Orange Book data, IQVIA market analytics, claims data
  Key Tools: IQVIA Market Analytics, FDA ANDA Database, Hatch-Waxman Calculator
  Sample Output: Generic competitor count per drug, market share transition curves, 
  time-to-generic analysis by therapeutic class.

Stage 4: Competitive Scoring & MoA Saturation Algorithm
  Purpose: Apply multi-dimensional scoring to rate MoA categories by generic 
  crowding intensity.
  Key Inputs: Generic penetration metrics, patent exclusivity, market intelligence
  Key Tools: HHI Calculator, Multi-Dimensional Saturation Scorer, Pricing Forecaster
  Sample Output: MoA saturation ranking (0-100), Herfindahl index, patent cliff risk.

Stage 5: Synthesis, Competitive Mapping & Recommendation Generation
  Purpose: Synthesize findings into strategic competitive maps and evidence-based 
  recommendations.
  Key Inputs: MoA rankings, unmet medical need, trial landscape, payer intelligence
  Key Tools: Competitive Landscape Mapper, Trial Analyzer, Recommendation Generator
  Sample Output: Strategic positioning map, executive summary, top 5 recommended 
  MoA focus areas.

Stage 6: Validation & Expert Consensus Review
  Purpose: Validate outputs against expert clinical and commercial judgment.
  Key Inputs: Complete outputs, expert panel feedback, historical outcomes
  Key Tools: Expert Panel Coordinator, Confidence Scorer, Assumption Challenger
  Sample Output: Validated rankings with confidence tiers, expert consensus report, 
  assumption sensitivity analysis.

KEY DELIVERABLES:
- MoA Saturation Ranking Table (all CNS targets ranked by generic crowding)
- Competitive Intensity Dashboard with visualizations
- Patent Cliff Risk Timeline (5, 10, 15-year forecast)
- Strategic Recommendation Letter (top MoAs to pursue/avoid)
- Market Access Strategy Brief (formulary placement, pricing strategies)

PIPELINE_141.docx
=================
PROMPT: "Predict pricing potential for rare genetic CNS target."

STAGE SUMMARY:
Stage 1: Rare Disease Epidemiology & Patient Population Mapping
  Purpose: Establish precise epidemiological characterization of rare genetic 
  CNS targets including disease incidence, prevalence, and geographic variation.
  Key Inputs: Orphanet, OMIM, Medicare/Medicaid, patient registries, literature
  Key Tools: Orphanet API, OMIM Database, Claims Parser, Registry Integrator
  Sample Output: Prevalence/incidence estimates by geography, diagnosed patient 
  populations, treatment eligibility calculations.

Stage 2: Orphan Drug Precedent Identification & Benchmark Pricing Extraction
  Purpose: Identify comparable orphan drugs and extract their pricing strategies 
  and reimbursement outcomes.
  Key Inputs: FDA/EMA orphan designations, pricing data, manufacturer documents
  Key Tools: FDA Orphan Portal, EMA Register, IQVIA Pricing, Comparable Drug Selector
  Sample Output: Orphan drug pricing catalog, US/EU/Japan price comparisons, 
  pricing range by therapeutic modality.

Stage 3: Cost-of-Illness & Willingness-to-Pay Analysis
  Purpose: Establish health economic framework by quantifying disease burden and 
  patient/payer willingness-to-pay.
  Key Inputs: Published health economic studies, claims data, utility weights, WTP surveys
  Key Tools: PubMed Literature Search, MarketScan Claims, EQ-5D Utilities, WTP Meta-Analysis
  Sample Output: Cost-of-illness estimates, QALYs lost per patient per year, 
  patient-derived price ceilings.

Stage 4: Pricing Model Development & Scenario Analysis
  Purpose: Synthesize data into comprehensive pricing models generating base case, 
  upside, and downside scenarios.
  Key Inputs: Epidemiology, precedent data, health economics, clinical benefit, 
  manufacturing costs.
  Key Tools: Value-Based Pricing Model, Orphan Disease Algorithm, Market Access Simulator
  Sample Output: Base case US pricing ($X/patient/year), geographic pricing strategy, 
  peak sales projections by scenario.

Stage 5: Market Access Strategy & Reimbursement Planning
  Purpose: Develop comprehensive market access strategy translating pricing into 
  actionable reimbursement plans.
  Key Inputs: Pricing recommendations, payer policies, clinical data, patient advocacy input
  Key Tools: Payer Policy Database, Reimbursement Dossier Builder, Payer Engagement Roadmap
  Sample Output: US/EU/Japan reimbursement strategy, health economic dossier templates, 
  payer engagement timeline.

Stage 6: Pricing Recommendation Validation & Sensitivity Testing
  Purpose: Validate pricing recommendations through expert review and sensitivity testing 
  of key assumptions.
  Key Inputs: Pricing models, expert feedback, historical outcomes, sensitivity analyses
  Key Tools: Sensitivity Analysis Engine, Real-World Outcomes Validator, Expert Coordinator
  Sample Output: Final validated pricing with confidence tiers, contingency strategies, 
  break-point analysis.

KEY DELIVERABLES:
- Epidemiological Summary (prevalence, incidence, geographic variation)
- Comparable Orphan Drug Pricing Matrix (US, EU, Japan comparisons)
- Cost-of-Illness & QALY Estimates (annual per-patient health burden)
- Pricing Scenarios (base case, upside, downside with revenue projections)
- Market Access Strategy Brief (reimbursement pathways by region)
- Final Pricing Recommendation (with confidence levels and risk factors)

  Sample Output: Clinical program count by target, patent landscapes, competitor programs,

  Key Inputs: Target recommendations, expert feedback, historical outcomes
  Key Tools: Expert Panel Coordinator, Evidence Quality Scorecard, Mechanism Tractability Reviewer
  Sample Output: Expert consensus validation report, confidence tier assignments, 
  final validated target recommendation list.

- Target Opportunity Ranking (genetic support + competitive white space)
